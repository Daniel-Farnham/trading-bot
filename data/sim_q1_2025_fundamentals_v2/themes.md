# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure & Custom Silicon [5]
Hyperscalers are building custom AI chips at scale, driving multi-year demand for custom ASICs, networking silicon, and AI-optimized data center infrastructure. This is a 3-5 year capex supercycle with AVGO, NVDA, TSM, and ARM as primary beneficiaries. As AI moves from training to inference at scale, networking becomes the critical bottleneck.

---

## US Energy Dominance (Trump) [4]
Trump's energy policy prioritizes domestic production, LNG export expansion, deregulation of fossil fuels, and nuclear revival for data center power. Russia halting gas to Europe accelerates European demand for US LNG. Traditional energy benefits from regulatory rollback. Nuclear getting a second look as AI power demand surges.

---

## Financial Deregulation & Capital Markets [4]
Trump administration expected to roll back banking regulations enabling M&A activity, embrace crypto through a friendlier SEC, and reduce capital requirements for banks. Financial sector and crypto-adjacent companies benefit from reduced regulatory headwinds and potential M&A boom.

---

## AI Power Demand [2]
AI data centers require massive and growing electricity consumption that didn't exist at scale before 2023. GPU clusters running inference at hyperscale need reliable 24/7 baseload power. This is driving a renaissance in nuclear power (long-term PPA agreements with tech companies), natural gas peaking capacity, and forcing utilities and independent power producers into a structural growth cycle. VST and CEG are the primary independent power beneficiaries. This is distinct from AI Infrastructure (silicon/networking) — it captures the physical energy layer of the AI stack and is supported by Trump's pro-nuclear, pro-gas policy agenda simultaneously.
