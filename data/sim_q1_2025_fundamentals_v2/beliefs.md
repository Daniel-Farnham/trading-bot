# Investment Beliefs

## Institutional Flow Primacy [3]
OBV (On-Balance Volume) is the primary decision signal for all thesis-driven positions because it reflects actual institutional capital flows — not sell-side sentiment, not RSI levels, not analyst price targets. Four operational rules: (1) ENTRY: Rising OBV + thesis alignment justifies entry even when MACD is bearish — institutions are accumulating regardless of short-term momentum. (2) HOLDING THROUGH OVERBOUGHT: After earnings confirm the thesis, hold through RSI up to 80 as long as OBV continues rising. Only reduce when RSI exceeds 80 AND OBV begins falling simultaneously. (3) ANALYST SIGNALS: When a major sell-side downgrade triggers a selloff, check OBV immediately. Rising OBV during an analyst-driven selloff = institutions are buying what retail is selling — hold the thesis. (4) STOPS EXCEPTION: Pre-committed stop levels are honored regardless of OBV direction. Stops reflect the risk budget established before position bias forms. OBV cannot override stops because we cannot know when 'institutions are wrong' until the loss is unrecoverable.
