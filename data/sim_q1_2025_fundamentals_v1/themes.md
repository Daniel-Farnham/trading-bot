# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure & Semiconductor Buildout [3]
The AI capex supercycle is accelerating with no visible slowdown. Hyperscalers are committing record capital to chips, networking, and data centers. This creates durable multi-year demand for custom silicon designers (AVGO), GPU makers (NVDA), foundries (TSM), and cloud platforms. Earnings confirmation and partnership announcements continue to validate the theme.

---

## Nuclear & Power Renaissance [1]
AI data centers require massive 24/7 clean baseload electricity that only nuclear can reliably provide at scale. US nuclear operators are restarting plants, signing multi-decade PPAs with tech giants, and receiving bipartisan political support. This is a decade-long structural theme with very few investable large-cap assets, creating scarcity premium.

---

## Trump Policy Regime Change [4]
The incoming Trump administration creates clear sector winners and losers. Winners: fossil fuels (deregulation, LNG export expansion), traditional financials (reduced regulatory burden), Big Tech (reduced antitrust enforcement), defense. Losers: clean energy (IRA rollback risk), ESG-driven utilities, heavily regulated consumer finance. Portfolio must account for this regime shift.

---

## Higher-for-Longer Rate Pressure [1]
Strong jobs data eliminates near-term Fed rate cut expectations. This sustains pressure on rate-sensitive assets: utilities, REITs, telecoms, and high-debt growth companies. Creates short opportunities in rate-sensitive businesses with structural headwinds and long opportunities in companies that benefit from higher rates or are rate-insensitive.

---

## Healthcare / GLP-1 Revolution [2]
The GLP-1 obesity and diabetes drug revolution represents a structural multi-decade pharmaceutical opportunity led by LLY and NVO. Obesity drug demand is secular and global, with penetration rates in early innings outside the US. Manufacturing capacity expansion is the key bottleneck, not demand, creating durable pricing power for the category leaders over a decade-long adoption curve.
