# Investment Beliefs

## Disciplined Deployment: Patience at Start, Aggression on Catalysts [3]
Capital deployment timing is a primary driver of returns. Two principles work in tandem: (1) In the first week of a new portfolio or major regime change, preserve 60%+ cash and open only 3-4 positions — the market reveals its true regime over the following 2-3 reviews, and early over-deployment in a misread environment is the primary risk to compounding. (2) When high-conviction macro catalysts materialize (new administration executive orders, major policy pivots), deploy aggressively into the most directly affected sectors within the first 72 hours — do not wait for earnings confirmation when the policy signal is unambiguous. Patient capital in uncertainty; aggressive capital when catalysts are real.

## Government Policy as Binary Thesis Events [3]
When government action directly impacts a position's thesis — tariff imposition, exemption, or pause; export controls; government contract awards or cancellations — treat the event as an immediate binary thesis evaluation trigger requiring same-review-cycle action, not background noise. Three-step response framework: (1) Classify permanence: permanent actions (export controls, contract awards) require full thesis reassessment; temporary actions (tariff pause) require profit-taking discipline before the relief rally dissipates. (2) Assess directionality: does the action validate the thesis (government contract for a LONG) or invalidate it (tariff exemption for a tariff-exposure SHORT)? (3) Act proportionally: thesis invalidation = full exit; partial thesis support shift = reduce position. This principle applies symmetrically — a government contract validating a company's capabilities is as strong a bullish signal as a tariff exemption is a bearish signal for a tariff-short thesis.
