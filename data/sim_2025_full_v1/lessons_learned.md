# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [5]
Presidential inauguration week executive orders are high-conviction entry points for policy-sensitive sectors (energy, financials, defense). The first 72 hours of a new administration reveal which campaign promises are real — when executive orders match the thesis on day one, accelerate deployment into those sectors immediately rather than waiting for earnings confirmation.

## Lesson 2 [1]
When a company you are SHORT raises its dividend AND announces a large buyback in the same earnings report, treat it as a thesis invalidation signal regardless of the long-term structural narrative. Management's decision to return capital aggressively signals confidence in cash generation that contradicts a structural decline thesis. Cover immediately and reassess.

## Lesson 3 [1]
CEO insider selling at-or-below your cost basis is an immediate exit trigger regardless of macro thesis strength. When the person with the most non-public information about a company's prospects sells shares at or below your entry price, it represents the highest-confidence institutional conviction reversal available. Apply this rule without exception — the CEO's sell action supersedes the macro narrative. Trigger: any Form 4 filing showing open-market CEO sale at a price at or below your entry.

## Lesson 4 [5]
Bear Market Regime Detection: When 3+ portfolio positions simultaneously show triple distribution signals (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling) across uncorrelated sectors, treat this as a macro regime-shift — not isolated stock failures. Trigger: 3+ simultaneous triple-distribution readings in a single review cycle. Action: close all triple-distribution positions immediately, initiate 2-3 short positions in structurally broken companies, raise cash to 50%+, concentrate remaining longs exclusively in names with OBV rising. The simultaneous nature across sectors indicates the regime, not individual company problems.

## Lesson 5 [1]
When a company you are SHORT wins a major government or military contract, treat it as an immediate thesis invalidation trigger regardless of long-term structural decline narrative. Government contract awards represent sovereign/institutional validation of the company's operational capabilities and provide guaranteed future revenue visibility — both directly contradict a 'structurally broken' thesis. Trigger: any major government/DoD contract announcement while holding a SHORT position. Action: evaluate exit within the same review cycle before technicals can reverse further against the position.

## Lesson 6 [3]
Presidential tariff announcements targeting a specific sector are immediate thesis-evaluation triggers for all positions in that sector. Action: (1) Assess each LONG in the targeted sector for direct manufacturing exposure — companies with >40% international production of targeted goods face the highest margin/volume risk. (2) Pure-play international manufacturers (NVO) and companies with majority overseas production of targeted products (LLY in Ireland) become SHORT candidates. (3) Companies that compete against tariffed goods or benefit from protected pricing can become LONG opportunities. Trigger: any presidential statement announcing sector-specific tariff imposition. Apply within the same review cycle before the market prices in the full impact.

## Lesson 7 [2]
Tariff exemptions for a sector are immediate SHORT exit triggers when the short thesis was built on tariff exposure risk — mirror of Lesson 6. When a president explicitly exempts a sector from tariffs, any short position opened on that sector's tariff exposure is thesis-invalidated. Action: cover within the same review cycle and lock in gains before the market fully prices in the exemption. Trigger: any presidential announcement exempting a specific sector from tariff application while holding a SHORT in that sector. Applied this week: LLY short covered at +10.6% on pharmaceutical exemption announcement.

## Lesson 8 [2]
A temporary tariff pause (not a permanent exemption) for countries where a SHORT company's manufacturing is based is functionally equivalent to Lesson 7 for exit purposes. A 90-day pause creates an immediate relief rally that can erase short gains just as permanently as an exemption. Trigger: any presidential tariff pause announcement that covers the primary manufacturing country of a SHORT position where tariff exposure was the thesis driver. Action: take profits on the short within the same review cycle — do not wait to see if the pause becomes permanent. The asymmetry is unfavorable: you lose gains quickly in the relief rally, but waiting to confirm permanence costs more than early exit.

## Lesson 9 [2]
US government export controls on semiconductors (chip bans to specific countries 'for the indefinite future') are structurally equivalent to permanent tariffs — they represent irreversible revenue impairment, not temporary headwinds. Unlike a tariff that can be negotiated away, export controls framed as indefinite technology denial require immediate TAM reduction in valuation models. Trigger: any USG export control announcement on a key product category to a major revenue market. Action: immediately reassess any LONG positions in affected companies, reduce AI Semiconductor theme conviction, and evaluate the company-specific impact on forward earnings before the market fully reprices.

## Lesson 10 [5]
When a SHORT position's technicals fully reverse simultaneously — MACD shifts bullish AND OBV shifts from falling to rising — cover immediately regardless of the fundamental thesis. Technical reversal on a short indicates institutional covering and/or accumulation beginning; continued holding increases losses asymmetrically as momentum builds against you. The fundamental story may remain intact but the market's vote supersedes the narrative. Trigger: MACD bearish→bullish flip + OBV falling→rising shift while holding a SHORT. Action: cover in the same review cycle, accept the loss, and reassess whether to re-enter short at a better technical setup.

## Lesson 11 [1]
When a high-conviction LONG shows MACD bearish + OBV falling (two distribution signals but NOT triple — still above SMA50) and a major earnings catalyst is within 1-2 weeks, hold through the technical weakness rather than applying the standard two-signal exit. Institutional distribution ahead of earnings often reflects hedging and profit-taking, not thesis abandonment. Trigger: LONG above SMA50 with MACD bearish + OBV falling AND earnings within 14 days. Action: suspend the two-signal exit evaluation until post-earnings — the fundamental catalyst takes precedence. NVDA this week: held through MACD bearish + OBV falling, double beat validated thesis comprehensively.

## Lesson 12 [1]
When the Fed pivots to active rate cuts, any SHORT position whose thesis was explicitly predicated on 'higher-for-longer' rates faces immediate thesis invalidation — treat the rate cut announcement as a binary policy trigger equivalent to a tariff exemption (Lesson 7). Rate cut cycles structurally compress REIT discount rates, expand multiples, and relieve refinancing pressure on leveraged entities — directly reversing the mechanics of a rate-sensitive short. Trigger: Fed rate cut announcement while SHORT a leveraged REIT or other entity where sustained elevated rates were the explicit thesis mechanism. Action: cover within the same review cycle before the rate-relief rally fully prices in the policy shift. This is a monetary policy analogue to Lessons 7-8 (tariff relief triggers).
