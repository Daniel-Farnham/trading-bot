# Investment Themes

## Trump Deregulation & Pro-Business Policy [2]
New administration confirmed Jan 6. Expect financial deregulation (Basel III rollback, CFPB neutering), energy permit acceleration, M&A approval revival, and potential corporate tax cuts. Bullish for banks, energy, and domestic industrials.

---

## AI Semiconductor Supercycle [5]
Hyperscaler AI capex remains at record levels. NVDA, AVGO (custom ASICs), TSM (manufacturing), MU (HBM memory) are primary beneficiaries. This cycle has more runway as inference workloads scale. Watch for pullbacks as entry points.

---

## Tariff Shock / Trade War Consumer Bifurcation [5]
Trump's sweeping global tariffs create a structural bifurcation: exempt sectors (semiconductors, pharma, oil) become defensive safe havens; exposed sectors (apparel, home furnishings, consumer imports) face margin compression and demand destruction; domestic value retailers benefit from consumer trade-down. This regime drives shorts in high-import-exposure consumer companies and longs in tariff-exempt or domestically-advantaged businesses.

---

## AI Power Infrastructure [4]
The physical power infrastructure required to run AI compute at scale is a distinct investment theme from semiconductors. AI data centers require unprecedented power density — NVDA's 800VDC architecture mandates new power delivery systems (Vertiv, Navitas); data center operators are signing long-term power purchase agreements with utilities; nuclear power is being revived specifically for AI baseload. Beneficiaries: power equipment makers (Vertiv), nuclear utilities (CEG), renewable utilities with AI contracts (NEE, VST), and power semiconductors (Navitas). This theme sits below the AI semiconductor layer in the value chain.
