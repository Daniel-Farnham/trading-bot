# Investment Beliefs

## Technical Confirmation Before Shorting [3]
Never short a stock that is still trading above its SMA50 with rising RSI and bullish MACD, regardless of how compelling the bear thesis appears. The market can remain irrational longer than a position can remain solvent. Wait for confirmed technical breakdown — below SMA50, declining RSI (ideally below 50), and bearish MACD — before entering short positions. This applies even to structurally declining businesses. Momentum kills premature shorts.

## Rate-Regime Portfolio Alignment [3]
The interest rate regime is the single most important macro variable for sector allocation. In a rising-rate environment: actively overweight financials (NIM expansion), energy (real asset pricing power), and cyclicals (operating leverage); actively underweight or avoid long-duration assets (clean energy utilities, high-multiple growth tech) which face valuation compression from rising discount rates. The rate environment can overwhelm a fundamentally sound company-level thesis for multiple quarters. Execute rotation preemptively when the rate trend is confirmed — not reactively after compression has occurred. Signal to act: 10-year yield in sustained uptrend with Fed taper/hike language.

## Conviction Compounds on Confirmation [3]
When a core position receives fundamental confirmation — earnings beat with above-consensus guidance, simultaneous price target raises from multiple independent major institutions, or direct policy catalysts materializing — increase position size if (1) technical conditions support entry (RSI not extended, MACD bullish or neutral, above SMA50) and (2) allocation limits allow. Confirmation reduces asymmetric uncertainty and increases thesis conviction. The optimal behavior is to add at confirmation, not wait for the next confirmation. Failure to act on confirmation is a form of opportunity cost that compounds across multiple positions.
