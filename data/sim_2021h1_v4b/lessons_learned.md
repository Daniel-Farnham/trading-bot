# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [1]
Policy catalysts (elections, legislation) can be the single largest near-term driver of sector rotation. When a clear political outcome occurs, reprice immediately — don't wait for earnings confirmation. The market front-runs policy, not actuals.

---

## Lesson 2 [1]
First deployment should be in highest-conviction, longest-duration theses where short-term volatility is acceptable. Reserve 60%+ cash to add on pullbacks and capitalize on later opportunities with better information.

---

## Lesson 3 [5]
Avoid shorting into strong momentum even if the long-term thesis is bearish — wait for technical confirmation of trend breakdown (below SMA50, declining RSI, bearish MACD) before entering short positions.

---

## Lesson 4 [5]
When a core long position reports earnings beats with above-consensus guidance, consider adding to the position (if technicals permit and allocation limits allow) rather than passively holding. Fundamental confirmation is the strongest signal to increase size. TSM's Q1 guide above estimates is exactly this signal — the market rewards acting on confirmation, not waiting for the next confirmation.

---

## Lesson 5 [2]
Market-wide deleveraging events driven by non-fundamental causes (e.g., meme stock short squeezes forcing hedge fund margin calls) create indiscriminate selling pressure across quality positions. Before reacting to a broad portfolio drawdown, identify the source of selling. If it is macro/technical contagion rather than thesis-specific deterioration, treat the weakness as a potential add opportunity. The signal to act is: multiple quality positions down simultaneously with no negative fundamental news for any of them.

---

## Lesson 6 [3]
When a short position reaches RSI < 30 and trades near the lower Bollinger Band, take partial profits even if the structural bear thesis remains fully intact. Extreme oversold conditions produce near-term bounces with high reliability. Locking in a portion of gains protects against the bounce while allowing the thesis to play out with reduced position. Re-add on the bounce if price fails to reclaim SMA50.

---

## Lesson 7 [5]
Rising interest rates create a bifurcated market in real-time: financials, energy, and cyclicals (benefiting from steeper yield curve) outperform while long-duration assets (clean energy utilities, high-multiple growth tech) face valuation compression from rising discount rates. When the 10-year yield is in a sustained uptrend, actively rotate toward value/cyclicals and reduce exposure to rate-sensitive long-duration plays — even when the fundamental thesis for those names is intact. The rate environment can overwhelm a sound thesis for quarters.

---

## Lesson 8 [5]
When a long position reaches RSI > 75 while simultaneously trading near the upper Bollinger Band and hitting 52-week highs, take partial profits (30-40% of position) even when the structural bull thesis is fully intact. Overbought conditions at resistance produce mean reversion with high historical reliability. The playbook: trim the extended portion, hold the core, and re-add the trimmed shares when RSI pulls back to the 55-65 zone with thesis confirmation. This is the long-position analog to Lesson 6 (partial profit-taking on oversold shorts).

---

## Lesson 9 [2]
When re-entering a previously stopped-out position on a strong earnings beat, the critical near-term confirmation variable is OBV direction in the 5-10 trading days post-earnings. If OBV continues falling despite strong results, institutions are distributing into the strength (sell-the-news dynamic) — this elevates stop-loss probability significantly and signals the position requires a tighter stop and smaller initial size. Only upgrade confidence from 'medium' to 'high' once OBV confirms institutional accumulation is resuming post-earnings.
