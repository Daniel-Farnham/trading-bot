# Investment Beliefs

## Regime Change Creates Sustained Sector Winners — Deploy Early and Hold Through Volatility [3]
When a fundamental macro regime changes — new administration, structural supply shock, multi-year policy shift — the winning sectors experience sustained multi-quarter repricing that rewards early positioning over consensus timing. The pattern is consistent: (1) structural catalysts affect entire industries, not single companies, creating broad tailwinds that compound; (2) institutional OBV accumulation during the entry phase confirms smart money agrees before consensus catches up; (3) early policy actions (CFPB freeze, LNG terminal approvals) prove the thesis is real, not speculative, creating a second confirmation leg to hold through. The key mistake to avoid is taking profits too early when institutional confirmation is just beginning — their rotation means the thesis has more runway, not less. Patience is rewarded: regime change trades take quarters to fully price in, and the biggest gains often come in the second and third legs after initial positioning.
