## Lesson 1
Geopolitical escalation (Middle East tensions) creates both risk and opportunity — use it to add hedging positions (energy) rather than panic-selling growth names. The VIX spike is a feature, not a bug, when you have 64% cash to deploy.

---
## Lesson 2
Geopolitical event-driven theses have short shelf lives — when entering a position based on escalating tensions (like XOM on Middle East conflict), set tighter timeframes and be prepared for rapid de-escalation. Diplomatic breakthroughs can unwind months of tension overnight. Consider using options for geopolitical plays instead of equity positions to limit downside while maintaining exposure.

---
## Lesson 3
When a position thesis is tied to a specific geopolitical event (CRWD/Iran cyber threat), track whether the catalyst is still active in weekly reviews. If the original catalyst fades but the position hasn't hit its stop, reassess whether the remaining 'secular' thesis alone justifies the allocation — don't let thesis drift keep you in a weakening position.

---
## Lesson 4
When a position exceeds its original price target (NVDA +32%, GOOGL +19%), don't just passively hold — actively reassess and either raise the target with updated thesis or take partial profits. Letting winners run without updated targets leads to thesis drift and makes it harder to evaluate risk/reward objectively.

---
## Lesson 5
In quiet news weeks with no macro catalysts, resist the urge to trade. Consolidation periods are when patient investors maintain discipline. The absence of news IS the signal — hold positions, take partial profits on winners that hit targets, and wait for better entry points on new ideas.

---
## Lesson 6
Trump's unprecedented move to fire a Fed governor signals that political risk to institutional independence is a real market factor to monitor. If Fed independence is credibly threatened, consider adding gold/commodity exposure or reducing duration-sensitive positions. Trigger: any further Fed personnel changes or legislative attempts to restructure the Fed.

---
## Lesson 7
When a stock is at extreme overbought levels (RSI 83+) AND faces new structural regulatory headwinds (antitrust remedies), that's a clear partial-profit signal regardless of how strong the underlying thesis seems. The combination of stretched technicals + fundamental deterioration is higher conviction than either signal alone. Trigger: RSI > 80 + negative regulatory/structural news = reduce by at least 30%.

---
## Lesson 8
When a major partner (OpenAI) signs a massive infrastructure deal with a competitor (Oracle), reassess any thesis built on 'exclusive partnership' advantages. Distribution moats (Copilot, Office) are stickier than supply-chain moats (cloud hosting). Trigger: any time a key partner announces significant deals with competitors, downgrade the 'partnership advantage' portion of the thesis and evaluate whether remaining thesis legs justify the position.

---
## Lesson 9
When a quiet news week coincides with a position at extreme RSI (85+) and 49% above entry, that's the ideal time to trim — low volatility means better execution on profit-taking. Don't wait for a negative catalyst to reduce an overbought winner; the absence of bad news IS the window. Trigger: position >30% above entry + RSI >80 + VIX low = trim at least 40%.

---
## Lesson 10
When a position exceeds its target by 20%+ (GOOGL at $245 vs $200 target) and you've already noted it in prior reviews without acting, that's a process failure. Create a hard rule: once a position passes its target by >15%, you MUST either (a) raise the target with a written updated thesis or (b) reduce the position by at least 30% at the next weekly review. No third option of 'just watching.' Trigger: any position >15% above original target at weekly review.

---
## Lesson 11
In quiet consolidation weeks approaching earnings season, the best action is often position management (trimming winners at/above targets) rather than new entries. Use the low-volatility window to execute reductions at favorable prices before earnings volatility arrives. Trigger: quiet news week + multiple positions above targets + major earnings within 2-3 weeks = prioritize trimming over new entries.

---
## Lesson 12
In a quiet news week with no macro headlines, the absence of catalysts is itself a signal — use the calm to execute on position management rules (Lesson 10, Lesson 11) that you've been noting but deferring. The pre-earnings quiet period is the optimal execution window for trimming because bid-ask spreads are tighter and volatility is low. Trigger: quiet news week + positions flagged for trimming in prior reviews = execute trims immediately, don't wait for 'one more week of data.'

---
## Lesson 13
China sanctions risk is a portfolio-level concern when multiple positions (NVDA, MU, MSFT, GOOGL) share the same geopolitical exposure. When a single policy risk affects 4+ positions representing >20% of portfolio value, treat it as a concentration risk even if the individual position sizes are within limits. Trigger: any policy headline affecting 3+ current holdings = assess aggregate exposure and consider reducing the most vulnerable position by at least 20%.

---
## Lesson 14
When multiple mega-cap earnings (MSFT, GOOGL) beat estimates and raise AI CapEx guidance in the same week, look for the AI-theme stock that is being left behind at oversold levels — that's often the best risk/reward entry. The rising tide will eventually lift all boats in the cohort. Trigger: 2+ AI mega-cap earnings beats in same week + any AI-theme stock at RSI <35 = consider a 5-6% allocation entry.

---
## Lesson 15
When a key competitor or ecosystem partner (Apple) adopts your portfolio company's technology (Google Gemini for Siri), that's a thesis-strengthening event that may justify raising price targets — but it's also the kind of euphoric catalyst that tempts you to hold an already-overextended position. Combine target raises with partial profit-taking: raise the target AND trim into the good news. Trigger: major ecosystem adoption announcement for a position already >15% above target = raise target + trim at least 25% of position.

---
## Lesson 16
When you have 5+ lessons all pointing to the same action (trim LLY) and you still haven't done it across multiple reviews, the problem isn't knowledge — it's execution discipline. Create a hard automation rule: if RSI >80 AND price >target AND gain >30%, the trim is MANDATORY and non-negotiable at the next weekly review. No 'but the thesis is strong' override. Trigger: any position meeting all three conditions = auto-trim 40% minimum, no exceptions.

---
## Lesson 17
When NVDA reports blowout earnings confirming AI infrastructure demand, immediately review ALL AI-adjacent positions for thesis strengthening — the signal applies to MSFT (Azure/OpenAI), MU (memory supply), CEG (power demand), AMZN (AWS), and META (AI infrastructure spend). Use the post-earnings session to identify which oversold AI names to add to, not just celebrate the NVDA position. Trigger: NVDA earnings beat + raised guidance = within 24 hours, rank all AI-theme holdings by RSI and consider adding to the most oversold.

---
## Lesson 18
When 3+ positions simultaneously exceed their targets by >15% (GOOGL +60%, MU +24%, LLY +19%), treat it as a portfolio-level rebalancing event, not individual position decisions. Batch all trims into the same weekly review to avoid the 'I'll get to it next week' trap that Lessons 10-12 were written to prevent. Trigger: 3+ positions above target at any weekly review = execute ALL trims in that session, no exceptions.
