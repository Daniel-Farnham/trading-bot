# Investment Beliefs

## Political Regime Changes Are the Highest-Conviction Macro Catalyst [3]
When a new administration gains unified government control, sector rotation is immediate and persistent — financials, energy policy beneficiaries, and infrastructure plays re-rate within days of the confirmed outcome. The policy tailwind persists for years. Act on the confirmed catalyst, not the expectation. This overrides valuation concerns in the post-election window. Waiting for 'pullbacks' after a confirmed regime change is a mistake — the dislocation IS the entry.

## Extreme ADX + Rising OBV Is an Institutional Regime Signal That Overrides Short-Term Momentum Indicators [3]
ADX >45 combined with OBV rising represents institutional regime-shift accumulation — not retail momentum. RSI overbought (>75) and MACD bearish are noise in this context; they measure short-term momentum, not trend durability. Do not exit or fade a position with ADX >45 and OBV rising based on RSI or MACD signals alone. The exit trigger is ADX declining below 35 OR OBV turning negative for multiple consecutive weeks — not a single bearish MACD cross. Broad-based OBV deterioration during known systemic events (GME, forced liquidation) should not trigger exits; wait one to two weeks for the systemic effect to clear.
