# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure Buildout [5]
The AI compute buildout is the dominant structural theme of 2025. Hyperscalers are committing hundreds of billions to AI infrastructure — GPUs, custom silicon, and the data centers to house them. This benefits the entire semiconductor supply chain: chip designers (NVDA, AMD, ARM), foundries (TSM), and memory (MU). The theme is not about which AI model wins; it is about the hardware layer that makes AI possible.

## Bank Capital Return Cycle [2]
Federal Reserve stress test results released this week confirm the largest US banks are overcapitalized relative to regulatory minimums. Goldman Sachs SCB dropped from 6.1% to 3.4% — a dramatic improvement enabling a 33% dividend hike. BNY's SCB stays at 2.5% floor, M&T's CET1 drops to 7.2%. When regulatory constraints loosen, banks return capital via dividends and buybacks. This is a classic 'multiple expansion' setup: higher dividends attract income investors, buybacks reduce share count, and the improved capital story brings in growth investors.

## Industrial Manufacturing Revival [1]
Heavy industry and manufacturing are showing unusual technical strength: CAT RSI=81, NUE RSI=73, DE RSI=57 (but RevGr=+51.6%). This likely reflects reshoring momentum, infrastructure spending, and domestic manufacturing investment. CAT's equipment is needed for construction, mining, and energy infrastructure. NUE produces domestic steel. If tariff/trade policies are encouraging domestic production, this theme has multi-year legs.

## Managed Care Headwinds [2]
Centene withdrawing 2025 guidance signals the managed care sector is under pressure — likely from rising medical loss ratios as post-COVID healthcare utilization normalizes above historical baselines. This is potentially a multi-quarter headwind. Sector-wide guidance withdrawals often precede broad earnings cuts across competitors. UNH, CNC, and other managed care names may face similar pressure. This is a SHORT theme to monitor.

---

## Consumer Stress [5]
Premium and discretionary consumer spending is under structural pressure from a weakening jobs market (July jobs tanked materially), rising economic anxiety, and collapsing luxury goods (Gucci -25%, LVMH disappoints). Aspirational consumer spending — premium QSR, luxury adjacent, discretionary — is most vulnerable as the labor market cracks and household confidence deteriorates. This is a short-side theme: identify premium consumer names with thin margins, modest growth, and high consumer confidence sensitivity.

## Healthcare Quality Growth [4]
Broad institutional accumulation across high-quality healthcare names: JNJ (OBV rising, RSI=76, ADX=38), ABBV (OBV rising, RSI=73), REGN (OBV rising, MACD bullish, RevGr=+21.4%), ISRG (OBV rising, ADX=40), TMO (OBV rising). This coordinated signal suggests institutional rotation into defensive quality growth as AI tech names consolidate at all-time highs. Healthcare companies with durable pricing power (biologics, medical devices, specialty pharma) are the destination. This is distinct from our existing AI Infrastructure theme — it is a parallel structural rotation, not a competing view.
