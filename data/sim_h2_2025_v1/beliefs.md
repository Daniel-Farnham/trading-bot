# Investment Beliefs

## OBV is the decisive institutional signal — trust it over narrative [3]
On-Balance Volume is the single most reliable indicator of what institutions are actually doing versus what analysts are saying. When OBV diverges from narrative (falling OBV during bullish headlines = institutions distributing), trust OBV. The NVDA exit this week is the canonical example: Dan Ives is out with '$5 trillion' while institutions are net-selling confirmed by OBV falling. Triple distribution (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling) is the mandatory exit signal. OBV rising during a price dip is the mandatory hold signal. Never override OBV with narrative convenience.

## Elevated RSI entries require a specific institutional catalyst, not just momentum [3]
Entering at RSI above 75 is only justified when a specific, dated institutional catalyst explains the elevated buying: Fed SCB reduction → dividend increase, regulatory approval, earnings beat with raised guidance. The catalyst means institutions are responding to fundamental change, not chasing price. Without a named catalyst, elevated RSI is a timing risk and should be treated as 'wait for pullback.' Applying this prevents chasing moves that are already 80% complete.
