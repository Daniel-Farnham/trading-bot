# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure & Semiconductors [5]
The global AI buildout requires unprecedented semiconductor investment across the stack: AI-optimized GPUs (NVDA), advanced foundry capacity (TSM), and chip architecture IP (ARM). Hyperscaler capex on AI infrastructure is accelerating with no visible ceiling. Companies in this ecosystem share exceptional characteristics: high margins, revenue growth, near-zero debt, and sustained institutional accumulation. This is a 3-5 year secular theme with NVDA, TSM, and ARM as the highest-conviction expressions. Secondary plays include AMD, AVGO, MU, and SMCI.

## Financial Sector Capital Return [3]
The 2025 Fed stress test cycle is a turning point: dramatically reduced Stress Capital Buffers across major banks signal regulatory confidence in bank health and enable a wave of capital return to shareholders. Goldman Sachs SCB fell 6.1%→3.4% (dividend +33%), BNY Mellon raised its dividend, M&T Bank's CET1 requirement dropped to 7.2%. Banks are transitioning from capital accumulation to capital distribution. This creates a positive re-rating catalyst as income investors reprice yield profiles. GS, JPM, BLK, and SCHW are primary beneficiaries.

---

## Healthcare Bifurcation [4]
The healthcare sector is splitting into two diverging sub-sectors: (1) Managed care / insurance facing structural cost pressures from rising medical utilization and Medicaid policy uncertainty — Centene's guidance withdrawal is a warning signal for the group (UNH, CNC); (2) Innovative healthcare (surgical robotics ISRG, precision medicine VRTX, diagnostics TMO) with durable competitive moats and pricing power that is outperforming. Strategy: avoid or short managed care, selectively long innovative healthcare on pullbacks.
