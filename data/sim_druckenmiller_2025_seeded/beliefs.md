# Investment Beliefs

## Policy Catalysts Are Priced In Before They Land [3]
When a policy-driven thesis position has gained 8%+ AND the catalyst event is imminent or has just been implemented AND technicals are extended (RSI>70 or near upper BB), the market has front-run the announcement. Do not add capital at this stage. Hold existing position if the structural thesis remains valid, but expect muted or negative near-term price action. Post-implementation, technicals will typically fade (MACD bearish, OBV flat) as the trade unwinds. A new fundamental catalyst — not the original policy event — is required to re-accelerate. Reinforced this week: CFPB dropping the Zelle case against JPM is an explicit deregulation thesis confirmation, yet JPM is flat/down at entry price — the deregulation trade had already been priced in before the regulatory action arrived.

## Triple Distribution Requires Thesis Verification — Macro vs Company-Specific Exit [3]
Triple distribution exit discipline applies symmetrically to SHORTS: when a short position was entered on triple distribution signals and those signals reverse (MACD bullish, OBV rising), the entry rationale is invalidated and the short must be closed — regardless of whether the fundamental bear case still holds. The OBV reversal on a short position is the institutional accumulation signal, which directly contradicts the short thesis. Corollary: a short position with OBV now rising should be exited, even if the company still has fundamental weaknesses, because institutions have decided the price is attractive. This prevents holding losing shorts into momentum-driven squeezes.

## OBV Direction Is The Primary Institutional Signal For Position Sizing [3]
OBV direction overrides MACD and RSI when making sizing decisions. OBV rising during any price weakness — including macro selloffs, extended RSI, or bearish MACD prints — signals institutional accumulation and is the primary trigger to ADD or HOLD a position. OBV falling after a pyramid entry (where OBV rising was cited as the primary rationale) invalidates the pyramid sizing — reduce back to pre-pyramid core allocation while retaining fundamental thesis exposure. OBV rising alone justifies holding even when RSI is extended (>70) or MACD turns bearish. This is distinct from triple distribution (which requires three simultaneous signals for full exit) — OBV direction alone governs whether to increase or decrease sizing around a core position.
