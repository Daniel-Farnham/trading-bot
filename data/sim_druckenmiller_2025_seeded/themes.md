# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure Supercycle [5]
Multi-year GPU and data center buildout driven by hyperscaler capex commitments. NVDA, AVGO, TSM, AMD, ARM are the hardware supply chain. META, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN are the demand side. Cloud and enterprise AI adoption accelerating through 2025-2026.

---

## Electrification and Grid Modernisation [3]
Global shift to electrified transport, heating, and industrial systems driving massive grid infrastructure investment.

---

## Ageing Population and Health Innovation [5]
73M baby boomers entering peak healthcare consumption. GLP-1 weight-loss drugs (LLY, NVO) expanding TAM as insurance coverage grows. Managed care scale (UNH). Surgical robotics (ISRG). Biotech innovation (VRTX, REGN, ABBV). Multi-decade demographic tailwind.

---

## Geopolitical Uncertainty and Defence [4]
Rising global instability — US-China tensions, tariff regimes, Middle East conflict, Russia-Ukraine. Benefits defence contractors, domestic energy independence (XOM, CVX, LNG), gold, and companies with purely domestic revenue. Hurts import-dependent supply chains and globally exposed manufacturers.

---

## Nuclear Renaissance & AI Power Infrastructure [4]
AI data centers require reliable 24/7 baseload electricity that solar and wind cannot provide at scale. Nuclear is the only viable solution. Hyperscalers are signing multi-decade PPAs with nuclear operators at premium prices. Trump administration is openly pro-nuclear. This is a multi-year capital deployment cycle benefiting CEG, VST, and potentially NRG.

---

## Trump Policy Tailwinds — Energy & Deregulation [4]
Incoming Trump administration will lift LNG export moratorium, accelerate drilling permits, roll back clean energy mandates, and deregulate financial services. Primary beneficiaries: LNG, XOM, CVX, OXY for energy; JPM, GS for deal-making and financial deregulation. Risk: tariff retaliation creating macro drag.

---

## Consumer Bifurcation — Tariff Inflation vs Discount Value [2]
US-China tariffs are now creating measurable consumer price inflation at shelf level (Walmart reporting sharp price hikes) and in holiday goods supply chains ('fewer, costlier dolls at Christmas'). Lower-income consumers bear disproportionate burden as import-dependent discretionary goods inflate. This creates a structural bifurcation: discount retailers with domestic-resilient models (DG reporting +45.5% revenue growth) gain traffic while import-dependent consumer brands face margin compression. Distinct from Geopolitical Uncertainty theme — this is specifically about consumer spending divergence and the investable trade in domestic value retail vs import-dependent discretionary.
