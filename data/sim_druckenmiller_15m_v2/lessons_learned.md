# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [1]
In the first week of a new portfolio, open only 1-2 scouts (5-8% total) to test the regime before committing capital. The cost of being wrong early is low; the cost of being right and having space to add is high.

---

## Lesson 2 [5]
Overbought conditions (RSI>80) in strong trends (ADX>40) are NOT automatic sell signals — they can stay overbought for weeks. Size small on initial entry, wait for a consolidation or pullback before adding.

---

## Lesson 3 [5]
When a major political catalyst thesis is confirmed (election result, policy announcement), re-enter stopped-out positions immediately even at higher prices if the thesis has strengthened — the bulk of the move typically comes AFTER the event, not before. Being stopped out pre-event is risk management, not thesis invalidation. Trigger: position is in 'Watching' + major catalyst just confirmed + stock is above prior stop but RSI < 82.

## Lesson 4 [2]
When a major regulatory agency head with an adversarial posture departs (e.g., SEC Chair stepping down), the entire regulated sector undergoes a structural re-rating that plays out over months — not a one-time price bump. The departure signals a multi-year shift in enforcement posture, product approval pipeline, and institutional participation appetite. On confirmation of such a departure, add aggressively to highest-conviction positions in that sector. Trigger: confirmed departure or replacement announcement of agency head with known hostile policy stance toward a sector you hold.

## Lesson 5 [1]
When management guides the next quarter MORE THAN 10% below consensus in a stock you hold for a near-term catalyst thesis, EXIT immediately — do not wait for technical confirmation. Management has direct order book visibility that supersedes all technical indicators. The guidance miss IS the thesis invalidation event. Trigger: quarterly earnings call where next-quarter guidance midpoint is >10% below consensus estimate AND management specifically cites weakness in the exact market that underpins your thesis.

## Lesson 6 [1]
Mechanical demand catalysts (index inclusions, ETF rebalancing, options-related forced buying) have a defined lifespan of roughly 3-5 weeks from the effective date. The stock often peaks during or shortly after the inclusion window as passive funds complete mandatory purchases and speculative buyers exit. Plan to reduce or exit mechanically-driven positions within 4 weeks of the effective inclusion date, even if the fundamental thesis remains — the mechanical driver is gone and price must now sustain itself on fundamentals alone. Trigger: position held with index inclusion as a primary catalyst + more than 3 weeks since effective date + OBV is falling.

## Lesson 7 [4]
Technology efficiency shocks (breakthroughs that dramatically reduce cost of a core process) require split analysis by position type rather than uniform response: (1) Training-compute-dependent infrastructure (NVDA) faces real headwinds if training cost collapses; (2) Inference-at-scale custom silicon (AVGO XPUs, custom ASICs) may benefit via Jevons Paradox — cheaper models drive more AI adoption, more inference demand, more custom silicon need; (3) AI application layer companies (META) are direct beneficiaries — lower inference costs expand product margins. Use OBV during the panic selloff as the institutional read: rising OBV = smart money buying the dip, thesis likely intact; falling OBV = institutional exit, thesis may be broken. Do NOT react to the price move alone — react to what OBV tells you about who is selling. Trigger: any AI infrastructure selloff >10% on a single efficiency/cost breakthrough headline.

## Lesson 8 [1]
When a top-tier concentrated investor (Druckenmiller, Loeb, Ackman, Einhorn) publicly discloses they've taken a LARGE position (top-3 to top-5 holding) in a stock you already own, treat this as a high-conviction pyramid signal — not as a reason to wait. Their public disclosure reflects weeks or months of prior accumulation; the bulk of their position-building likely coincides with the OBV rises you've been observing. The disclosure confirms your institutional accumulation thesis and signals further buying ahead as others follow. Trigger: existing position + credible concentrated investor publicly announces stock is a top-5 holding + OBV is rising + RSI below 80.

## Lesson 9 [5]
Simultaneous triple distribution across multiple unrelated portfolio positions during an identifiable macro shock (tariff escalation, geopolitical crisis) is a portfolio-level regime signal — not stock-specific noise. When 3+ positions show below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling in the same review week during a clear macro catalyst, the correct response is: (1) close positions with played-out catalysts immediately, (2) reduce oversized positions by 30-40% to manage concentration risk, (3) hold only positions with OBV still rising, (4) do NOT open new positions until the macro shock resolves. The simultaneous nature of the distribution is the key — it signals institutional de-risking across the board, not individual thesis failures. Trigger: 3+ portfolio positions in triple distribution in same review week + identifiable macro shock headline.

## Lesson 10 [1]
Earnings Beat with OBV Falling = Sell-the-News Distribution: When a held position reports a strong earnings beat with forward guidance above consensus, but OBV is simultaneously falling, institutions are distributing into the news event. This is a sell-the-news pattern. Correct response: (1) thesis is confirmed by the fundamental beat — do NOT exit; (2) do NOT pyramid — OBV falling overrides the fundamental improvement as an entry timing signal regardless of how strong the beat; (3) wait for OBV to turn positive post-earnings before deploying any additional capital; (4) if OBV continues falling for 2+ reviews after the earnings event, reassess whether the smart money has structurally changed their thesis on the stock. Trigger: held position + earnings EPS and revenue beat + forward guidance above consensus + OBV falling at time of report.

## Lesson 11 [1]
Catastrophic stop rules are inviolable regardless of external validation pedigree. When a core position has (1) breached the 30% catastrophic loss threshold AND (2) shows triple distribution simultaneously, exit immediately — no analyst upgrade, no famous investor disclosure, no long-term thesis argument overrides these dual triggers. The endorsement was formed at a different price point and pre-tariff macro regime. The catastrophic stop exists precisely for regime-change black swan scenarios. Trigger: core position with 30% loss + triple distribution + identifiable macro regime change (tariffs, geopolitical shock, regulatory reversal).

---

## Lesson 12 [1]
Government policy-driven macro panics (tariff escalations, sanctions, regulatory threats) can reverse suddenly without warning, unlike structural market forces (technology disruption, demographic shifts, balance sheet deterioration). When a market decline is caused by a single discrete policy decision, classify each position's risk as POLICY RISK vs STRUCTURAL RISK before acting. Policy risk alone should NOT trigger exits if OBV remains rising — institutions who have longer holding periods look through policy noise. Only exit a policy-risk position if OBV confirms distribution (institutions also believe the policy change is permanent). Trigger: when a held position faces thesis pressure from a specific government policy announcement, determine whether the risk is policy-driven (potentially reversible) or structural (fundamentally changed) before deciding whether to reduce or exit.
