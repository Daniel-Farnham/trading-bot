# Investment Themes

## AI Infrastructure Buildout [4]
The AI compute and networking buildout is a multi-year capex cycle driven by hyperscaler competition. Despite softer consumer AI (iPhone 16 demand weakness), enterprise/cloud AI spending is accelerating. Key beneficiaries: NVDA, AVGO, TSM, MU. AVGO's custom silicon for Google/Meta is particularly defensible.

---

## Nuclear Renaissance [4]
AI data centers require 24/7 reliable baseload power that renewables cannot provide. Nuclear is the only scalable solution. Hyperscalers are signing long-term nuclear PPAs. CEG and VST are the primary equity beneficiaries in the US. This is a decade-long structural shift, not a trade.

---

## Trump Election Trade [1]
Trump leading in key swing states (Pennsylvania tied, leading in others). A Trump win likely means: energy deregulation (XOM, CVX, OXY), crypto deregulation (COIN), reduced regulatory burden on banks (JPM, GS), potentially weaker renewable energy subsidies (ENPH headwind). Need to monitor polls weekly.

---

## LNG Energy Export [1]
US LNG exports are a structural multi-year beneficiary of European energy security reorientation post-Russia. Trump deregulation will unlock new export terminal approvals that Biden blocked. Russian gas halt to Europe confirms the permanent demand shift toward US LNG supply.

---

## Healthcare Innovation / GLP-1 [3]
Structural multi-decade opportunity in obesity, diabetes, and metabolic disease treatment via GLP-1 agonists. Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk are primary beneficiaries. Pipeline expansion to cardiovascular, addiction, and potentially Alzheimer's applications could multiply total addressable market. Rising obesity rates globally plus aging demographics create durable demand independent of economic cycles. Counter-cyclical versus tech — provides critical portfolio diversification.

---

## Consumer Trade-Down [1]
In tariff-induced economic slowdowns and recessions, consumers trade down from mid-market and premium brands to value and discount retailers. Dollar General, Dollar Tree, and Walmart are structural beneficiaries as middle-class budgets tighten. This theme is counter-cyclical, uncorrelated to tech and AI macro volatility, and provides genuine portfolio diversification during economic stress cycles.
