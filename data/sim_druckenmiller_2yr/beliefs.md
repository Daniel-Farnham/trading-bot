# Investment Beliefs

## Overbought Patience in Confirmed Trends [3]
In confirmed uptrends (ADX>35), severely overbought conditions (RSI>78-80) near upper Bollinger Bands are poor entry points — even when the underlying thesis is compelling and intact. The trend survives the consolidation; the overextended entry absorbs the drawdown. Target RSI reset to the 55-65 range before initiating new positions, re-entering watched positions, or sizing up scouts. The thesis being valid is necessary but not sufficient — entry timing within the trend cycle materially affects risk-adjusted returns. Applies equally to new entries, re-entries, and position adds. Never chase a strong thesis into an overbought extreme; wait for the thesis to come to you.

## Portfolio-Wide Technical Weakness is Consolidation, Not Reversal [3]
When 5+ portfolio positions show MACD bearish and OBV falling simultaneously, the correct diagnostic is macro rotation vs. thesis break — not automatic exit. The test: is the weakness company-specific (negative catalyst, earnings miss, product failure) or market-wide (broad sector rotation, risk-off, macro event)? If company-specific catalysts are absent or positive while the market broadly distributes (evidenced by defensive rotation into overbought utilities and healthcare simultaneously), the technical signal is contaminated by macro noise. Correct response: hold, check OBV at next review, exit only if the COMPANY-SPECIFIC triple distribution persists after macro clears OR a thesis-breaking catalyst emerges. A position like LLY with positive CEO commentary and whale accumulation is a hold even when the chart shows triple distribution during a market-wide selloff.
