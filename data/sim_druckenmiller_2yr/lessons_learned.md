# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [5]
When a scout confirms thesis rapidly (>15% gain in first review period), resist two temptations: (a) taking profits early — the initial gain is confirmation, not the full move, and (b) adding aggressively at extended technicals (RSI >75, near upper BB). The correct action is hold existing scout with original stop intact, let the thesis play out to its catalyst, and wait for RSI reset to 55-65 range before sizing up. Scouts are thesis validators — let the mechanical stop manage risk and save capital for when the setup is cleaner.

## Lesson 2 [4]
Bitcoin ETF authorized participant naming is a high-probability leading indicator of imminent SEC approval — when major issuers (BlackRock, Fidelity) name their clearing and settlement participants (Jane Street, JPMorgan), approval typically follows within days to weeks. This creates a narrow but high-conviction re-entry window in crypto-adjacent equities before the catalyst fully prices in. Act on the mechanism, not the rumor.

## Lesson 3 [4]
Catalyst exhaustion after 'sell the news' events: When a binary, long-anticipated catalyst (Bitcoin ETF approval) finally occurs after months of speculation, prices often reverse sharply as event-driven traders exit and short-term holders take profits — even if the long-term thesis is intact. For positions held specifically around a known catalyst event, plan the exit BEFORE the event occurs. If the position has already appreciated materially in anticipation, reduce or exit 1-2 weeks prior. If holding through the event, set tight stops immediately upon the event to capture the initial move while protecting against reversal. The thesis surviving is not the same as the trade surviving.

## Lesson 4 [5]
Synchronized MACD bearish divergence across a portfolio of trend positions — when most holdings show MACD bearish simultaneously after a strong 6-8 week rally — is a consolidation phase signal, not a reversal signal, provided prices remain above SMA50 and at least some positions show OBV rising. The trap is mass-exiting on synchronized technical weakness and missing the continuation leg. Trigger: 5+ positions showing MACD bearish simultaneously. Correct response: check SMA50 and OBV on each position individually. Exit only when the triple signal is met (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling) on a specific name, not due to portfolio-wide sentiment.

## Lesson 5 [4]
AI chip export restrictions are a recurring, escalating policy risk for semiconductor hardware positions. The US government has imposed three rounds of restrictions since 2022, each progressively tightening the definition of 'advanced' chips banned from China export. For any semiconductor name with >15% China revenue exposure, underwrite a 15-25% addressable market haircut as a base case. This does not invalidate AI hardware theses whose demand is US/Western driven, but it should constrain confidence level: semiconductor hardware positions with China exposure are capped at 'medium' confidence (scout sizing) unless US-only demand analysis fully compensates for China revenue loss. Apply this check at thesis initiation for any chip position.

## Lesson 6 [4]
When a core position's original price target is exceeded due to a major binary catalyst event (FDA drug approval, transformative acquisition, regulatory win), the correct response is NOT to close based on the stale original target — it is to reassess the thesis with the new information and set an updated target reflecting the expanded opportunity. Original targets are set on incomplete information; catalysts change the probability distribution of outcomes. Closing LLY at $760 when donanemab approval was the exact catalyst embedded in the thesis would be thesis-driven selling replaced by arbitrary price anchoring. Trigger: core position exceeds original target because a named catalyst in the thesis fires. Action: update target, do not close.

## Lesson 7 [2]
Deeply-embedded enterprise security software (kernel-level, widely deployed) creates a dual-edged moat: high retention due to integration depth, but catastrophic blast radius when a faulty update is pushed globally. CrowdStrike's Falcon sensor grounding airlines and hospitals demonstrates that the same characteristic creating switching costs also creates a single point of failure across millions of enterprise endpoints simultaneously. Trigger: confirmed faulty update causing multi-sector, multi-country operational disruption at an enterprise security vendor. Action: treat as thesis-breaking event, not a buying dip — the litigation exposure, reputational damage, and enterprise procurement churn from a global outage of this magnitude takes multiple quarters to resolve. Do not confuse technical oversold conditions (RSI=19) with a buying opportunity when the reputational damage is structural.

## Lesson 8 [5]
When broad market distribution (OBV falling across 10+ unrelated stocks simultaneously) coincides with triple distribution signals on multiple portfolio positions, the correct diagnostic is: check whether the weakness is company/sector-specific (negative earnings, product failure, regulatory action) or market-wide rotation. If company-specific catalysts are absent or neutral-to-positive (as with LLY CEO's positive GLP-1 supply commentary during a market-wide selloff), the technical signal is contaminated by macro noise — it is not a thesis break. Trigger: portfolio shows triple distribution on 3+ positions simultaneously while company-specific news is neutral or positive. Response: hold positions, do not exit based on technicals alone, reassess if OBV remains falling after macro noise clears at next review.

## Lesson 9 [4]
ADX entry signal decay: High ADX (>40) at entry is a lagging indicator of past trend intensity, not a forward guarantee. ADX can collapse from 46 to 13 within 2-3 review periods after entry (observed with GS: ADX=46 at entry, ADX=13 now). When ADX decays rapidly post-entry, downgrade the technical conviction component of the thesis and rely more heavily on the macro/fundamental thesis to justify holding. Trigger: ADX drops >20 points within 2 review periods of entry. Action: reassess whether the institutional trend signal that drove entry conviction is still valid; if macro thesis holds, stay; if the thesis was primarily technical, reassess sizing.

## Lesson 10 [3]
Short positions with MACD turning bullish and OBV rising — even while the fundamental short thesis remains unresolved — signal a technical squeeze risk window. When a short's MACD transitions from bearish to bullish AND OBV begins rising without a corresponding thesis-breaking catalyst, the market is pricing in eventual thesis resolution (strike ending, approval, regulatory clearance). Trigger: short position shows MACD bullish + OBV rising for 2+ consecutive reviews while fundamental thesis remains intact but unresolved. Action: tighten stop to reduce squeeze exposure (move from original stop to 4-5% above current price), monitor news closely for early resolution signals. This is not a mandatory exit — the thesis may still play out — but absorbing a technical squeeze on a short has asymmetric downside that warrants defensive adjustment.

## Lesson 11 [2]
Post-election sector rotation creates widespread false triple distribution signals in defensive and healthcare sectors. When a major election triggers rapid regime rotation (defensive/healthcare → cyclical/financial/crypto), technical deterioration across healthcare, utilities, and bonds reflects systematic institutional reallocation rather than individual thesis breaks. Trigger: presidential election coincides with simultaneous healthcare/defensive triple distribution while financials/cyclicals/crypto surge in the same week. Action: apply heightened skepticism to exit signals in defensive/healthcare for 2-4 weeks post-election; require company-specific negative catalysts (earnings miss, pipeline failure, regulatory action) to confirm thesis break rather than relying solely on technicals during rotation period.

## Lesson 12 [1]
When a core catalyst driving a short thesis is resolved (strike ends, legal case settled, regulatory action lifted), exit promptly even if other thesis pillars remain. Trigger: a named catalyst in the short thesis resolves before the other bearish factors have fully played out. Action: reassess the weight of remaining pillars — if the resolved catalyst was the primary driver of the original confidence level, exit the short and take available profit rather than waiting for remaining pillars to play out. Risk/reward on a multi-pillar short degrades severely when the most visible near-term catalyst is removed, as a relief rally often follows regardless of remaining fundamental headwinds. Do not rationalize holding by citing surviving pillars if the core of the thesis is gone.

## Lesson 13 [2]
Macro panic OBV divergence is a higher-confidence accumulation signal than normal OBV rising: when a core holding shows OBV RISING during a broad market panic (2%+ single-day loss, $1T+ market cap destruction), the institutional conviction signal is stronger than if OBV rose on a calm day — it means real capital is being deployed into forced selling. JPM and CRM showing OBV rising during the Liberation Day $2T wipeout is more meaningful than the same signal on a flat tape. Trigger: broad market panic day (>2% S&P loss) with a core holding showing OBV rising. Action: treat as highest-conviction accumulation signal, resist the urge to exit even if P&L is negative.

## Lesson 14 [4]
Pharmaceutical tariff announcements require immediate geographic manufacturing audit of all healthcare positions. Import tariffs affect domestic and foreign pharma manufacturers asymmetrically: foreign manufacturers (NVO: Denmark, ROCHE: Switzerland) face direct cost exposure while domestic manufacturers (LLY: US buildout) may face neutral-to-positive competitive impact as foreign competition is repriced. Trigger: presidential pharmaceutical tariff announcement. Action: audit each healthcare position's primary manufacturing geography before deciding whether to hold or reduce — do not treat all pharma names identically.

## Lesson 15 [1]
Platform ad revenue concentration in Chinese seller spend (Temu, Shein) creates a specific policy risk: when US-China trade policy tightens and de minimis exemptions close, Chinese seller advertising budgets evaporate rapidly as their business model depends on those exemptions. Trigger: US executive action closing de minimis exemptions for Chinese goods. Action: audit META, GOOGL, SNAP exposure to Chinese seller ad spend as a percentage of total revenue before deciding whether to reduce or hold; if Chinese seller revenue represents less than 5% of total revenue, treat as manageable headwind rather than thesis break.
