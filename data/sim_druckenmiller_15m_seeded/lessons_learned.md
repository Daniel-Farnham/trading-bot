# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [4]
On week 1 of any new simulation, deploy maximum 1-2 scouts and keep 85%+ cash. The first review has the least regime context — let the market show its hand before committing capital. Patience in the first 2 reviews is not timidity; it is information gathering.

---

## Lesson 2 [5]
Analyst price target raises on existing positions within the first 2 weeks are among the strongest upgrade-to-core signals available. When a scout is down modestly in price but OBV is rising AND a credible sector analyst raises the PT significantly (Guggenheim VST to $177), this is the archetypal 'upgrade on dip with conviction' moment — the market is wrong short-term, institutions know it, and we should match institutional confidence rather than following short-term price action.

## Lesson 3 [2]
When a top-5 defense prime contractor (L3Harris, RTX, LMT, NOC, GD) announces a strategic partnership with a defense AI platform company, treat this as the highest-tier government spending thesis validator available. Prime contractors only commit to strategic partnerships when they have validated demand pipeline from government customers. This signal justifies holding through technical overextension (RSI 70+) as long as OBV remains rising and MACD deterioration is not confirmed by falling OBV.

## Lesson 4 [3]
When 4+ independent sell-side analysts raise price targets on a core position within a single week post-earnings (broad consensus upgrade), treat this as a top-tier thesis STRENGTHENING signal that overrides short-term bearish technicals (MACD bearish, OBV falling). Multiple independent analysts are reacting to the same Q3 data showing the thesis accelerating — price action will catch up to fundamentals within 2-4 weeks. Do not reduce or exit a core position in this scenario unless triple distribution also triggers.

---

## Lesson 5 [4]
When a scout position was opened for a binary catalyst (election, FDA decision, earnings) and the catalyst resolves in your favor near the target price, close the scout immediately and redeploy into the second-order beneficiaries of that macro event. The first-order trade gets priced in on event day; the alpha is in the second-order plays that institutions take 1-4 weeks to fully position. (JPM scout → COIN: financial deregulation first-order priced same day; crypto deregulation second-order still ramping.)

---

## Lesson 7 [4]
When the primary regulatory catalyst named at position entry explicitly materializes (e.g., SEC Chair Gensler confirmed stepping down when COIN was entered precisely because Trump committed to replacing him), the market is still partially pricing in the risk premium — the actual implementation (new SEC chair confirmed, enforcement actions dropped) has not yet occurred. This is the optimal hold signal: the thesis is confirmed but the full repricing is ahead. Do not take profits at this stage. The catalyst materialization is the beginning of the re-rating, not the end.

---

## Lesson 8 [5]
When a core position simultaneously shows triple distribution (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling) AND a new geopolitical or regulatory headwind emerges in the same review period, treat the combination as a high-conviction exit signal even if the underlying long-term thesis remains directionally valid. Technical breakdown signals institutions are already repricing the new risk — exit at a small loss rather than waiting for fundamentals to fully price in. Do not rationalize holding because 'the thesis is intact': the thesis may be intact in a 3-year frame but broken in a 3-month frame.

---

## Lesson 10 [3]
When a position shows triple distribution (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling) but the primary thesis catalyst is imminent within 1-2 weeks (inauguration, earnings, FDA decision), hold for one additional review period before triggering the technical exit. Pre-catalyst technical weakness often reflects profit-taking by shorter-horizon traders, not thesis breakdown by thesis-aligned institutional investors. If the catalyst passes and triple distribution persists without thesis confirmation, execute the exit immediately. This is a one-review grace period only — not a license to hold indefinitely through distribution.

---

## Lesson 11 [4]
When US AI hardware export sanctions are announced, immediately triage holdings into two buckets: (1) direct targets — companies whose revenue depends on exporting controlled chips to restricted markets (NVDA Hopper/Blackwell to China, AMD MI series); (2) insulated positions — custom silicon providers whose chips are designed exclusively for specific hyperscaler customers (AVGO XPU for Google/Meta/Apple), internal chip designers (GOOGL TPU), and demand-side hyperscalers (AMZN, META, MSFT) who simply buy from whoever isn't sanctioned. Do not exit insulated positions on AI chip sanction headlines — the supply-side constraint affects commodity chip vendors, not infrastructure builders or custom silicon operators. Sentiment spillover creates noise but not thesis invalidation for insulated names.

## Lesson 12 [4]
When a new administration publicly commits to a named, dollar-quantified infrastructure buildout with named private sector partners (e.g., Stargate AI $500B with SoftBank, Oracle, OpenAI), treat all existing positions exposed to that infrastructure theme as having their thesis LOCKED IN for the full stated commitment horizon. The public commitment creates a multi-year capex floor that short-term technicals cannot capture. Do not reduce or exit theme-aligned positions on technical noise when the policy and capital commitment is this explicit and this large — the commitment itself becomes the fundamental anchor.

## Lesson 14 [5]
When a broad macro shock (tariff regime change, credit crisis, geopolitical disruption) triggers simultaneous triple distribution across unrelated sectors in a single review period, do NOT apply mechanical triple-distribution exits universally. First assess whether the macro factor DIRECTLY threatens each specific position's revenue model in the 3-6 month horizon. A tariff shock breaks global manufacturers and consumer discretionary retailers but does NOT break domestic defense AI analytics, pharma with domestic demand, or domestic energy producers who may benefit from foreign competition tariffs. The correct triage question is: 'Does this macro event directly impair this company's specific revenue or cost structure in our stated time horizon?' Macro breadth creates noise; only thesis-specific impact justifies exit.

---

## Lesson 15 [5]
When 80%+ of tracked equities simultaneously enter triple distribution in a single review period, the high cash position is the primary portfolio asset — not an opportunity cost. The correct response is NOT broad deployment but hyper-selective accumulation in the 2-3 names showing OBV-rising divergence (institutional accumulation against the selloff). These OBV-positive outliers in a broad distribution event carry disproportionate signal: institutions with conviction are making asymmetric bets against the panic. Size these positions with confidence. Resist the urge to deploy cash broadly just because it 'feels like a buying opportunity' — the opportunity is only real where OBV confirms it.

---

## Lesson 16 [2]
Lesson 17: When a broad tariff regime is announced (Liberation Day equivalent), immediately bifurcate the entire universe through TWO independent lenses: (1) Revenue geography — is revenue domestic or export-dependent? (2) Manufacturing geography — is the cost base domestic or import-dependent? The highest-conviction accumulation targets are double-insulated businesses: domestic revenue AND domestic manufacturing (FSLR solar panels made in Ohio, AMT tower leases, UNH domestic insurance). The highest-conviction short candidates are double-exposed businesses: domestic revenue but import-dependent manufacturing (NKE selling in US but manufacturing in 46%-tariff Vietnam). This framework is additive to Lesson 14 (triage on revenue impairment) and Lesson 16 (export sanctions vs import tariffs are separate vectors) — it synthesizes them into a four-quadrant tariff exposure matrix. Apply this lens at the start of every review period following any major tariff announcement.

## Lesson 17 [5]
When a scout short position shows MACD turning bullish while OBV remains falling, this is a single counter-signal, not a cover trigger. The exit signal for a short requires triple accumulation (above SMA50 + MACD bullish + OBV rising — all three). A MACD reversal with OBV still falling means buyers are emerging but institutions are not absorbing them with conviction. Maintain the short thesis through single-signal noise; only cover when all three accumulation signals align simultaneously or when the fundamental thesis is formally broken by news.

---

## Lesson 18 [1]
When a company reports an EPS miss that is entirely attributable to a single identified one-time charge (regulatory compliance cost, export restriction write-down, legal settlement), always strip out the charge before evaluating business health and triggering any thesis review. NVDA Q1 reported $0.81 vs $0.88 estimate — a miss — but the entire shortfall was caused by the $4.5B H20 China export charge. Ex-charge EPS was $0.96, a clear beat. Revenue beat, Q2 guidance in line, demand 'incredibly strong.' A reported EPS miss driven by a disclosed, non-recurring, one-time charge is NOT evidence of business deterioration. Apply this filter before any technical or thesis reassessment following a headline EPS miss.
