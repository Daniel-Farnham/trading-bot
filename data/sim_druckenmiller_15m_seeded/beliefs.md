# Investment Beliefs

## Technical Extension Is Not Thesis Invalidation [3]
RSI 70+ or proximity to the upper Bollinger Band signals elevated pullback risk and must be flagged in every review. But these signals alone never justify exiting a position. The only valid exit signal from technicals is TRIPLE DISTRIBUTION: below SMA50 AND MACD bearish AND OBV falling — all three simultaneously. A bearish MACD with rising OBV means institutions are absorbing the selling; that is accumulation, not distribution. A single bearish signal (MACD alone, RSI alone) is noise within an ongoing trend. Hold through technical extension when OBV remains positive and the thesis is intact. The instinct to sell something because it 'looks extended' is the primary source of premature exits in winning trades.

## Macro Shock Triage Protocol [3]
When macro shocks occur (tariff announcements, geopolitical events, Fed surprises, trade regime changes), the mandatory first step is triage — not reaction. The triage question: Does this event directly impair this specific company's revenue OR cost structure in the 3-6 month horizon? Companies with confirmed direct impact showing triple distribution receive immediate exit signals (Lesson 8). Companies without direct impact should be held regardless of broad market selloff; OBV-positive names in those selloffs are highest-conviction accumulation targets (Lesson 15). Critical nuance: 'insulated' is not binary — a company can be insulated from one risk vector (export sanctions on customer geography) while exposed to another (import tariffs on manufacturing geography). Always evaluate both revenue geography and cost geography as independent risk vectors (Lesson 16).
