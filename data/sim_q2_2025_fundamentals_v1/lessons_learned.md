# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [3]
In tariff shock environments, immediately sort the universe by supply chain geography — companies with >50% Asia manufacturing face disproportionate downside regardless of brand strength. Initiate shorts early before consensus catches up.

---

## Lesson 2 [5]
When a major policy shock (tariffs, rate decision, geopolitical event) hits, the best technicals in the universe identify where institutional capital is already positioned defensively — focus the long book there rather than trying to catch falling knives in impacted sectors.

---

## Lesson 3 [5]
First-review deployment discipline: resist the urge to fully deploy in week one. The tariff situation will evolve over weeks; leave 60%+ cash to add to winners and open new positions as the macro picture clarifies.

---

## Lesson 4 [1]
In severe tariff shock environments, demand destruction can cause oil price collapse that directly contradicts the energy independence thesis. When global trade volumes fall sharply, the demand destruction channel (fewer ships, less industrial activity, lower growth) overwhelms the supply security premium. Trigger: when tariff shock is severe enough to cause equity market selloffs of 5%+, treat energy as a short candidate, not a long — and exit energy longs when technical stops are breached regardless of the long-term thesis.

---

## Lesson 5 [1]
Policy-driven positive catalysts (Medicare rate increases, regulatory approvals, government contract awards) can create explosive outperformance for defensive sector longs during broad bear markets — the combination of defensive positioning PLUS a positive fundamental catalyst is a double-engine trade with minimal tariff-related risk. Trigger: proactively scan for upcoming CMS/FDA/regulatory decisions as potential asymmetric upside events when holding healthcare longs in a market under macro pressure.

## Lesson 6 [5]
When a broad policy shock evolves into a more targeted regime (e.g., tariff pause for allies + China escalation), treat it as thesis refinement, not invalidation. Immediately re-evaluate all positions for China-specific vs. ally-country exposure and tighten the long/short bifurcation accordingly. The refined regime creates MORE actionable positioning, not uncertainty. Trigger: any time a broad policy shock is followed by partial rollback or carve-outs.

## Lesson 7 [2]
When a tariff exemption is announced then immediately walked back or reframed as temporary within 24 hours (as with electronics exemption April 11-13), treat the resulting relief rally as noise, not a thesis change. The underlying structural tariff impact on China-supply-chain companies is unchanged. Fade rallies in China-exposed stocks caused by ambiguous/contradictory policy signals — wait for clear, durable policy change before adjusting short positions. Trigger: any time administration messaging on tariff relief is contradicted within 48 hours.

## Lesson 8 [2]
When US export control language specifies 'indefinite' license requirements for technology products to China (as with NVIDIA H20), treat the affected China revenue as permanently impaired — not subject to tariff negotiation or rollback. This is categorically different from goods tariffs: technology access bans create hard barriers that cannot be resolved through trade deals. Trigger: any time export control notice includes the word 'indefinite' — immediately reassess all positions with China technology revenue exposure and treat as structural shorts or exits.

## Lesson 9 [5]
Hard stop prices are contracts, not suggestions. UNH was given a $488 stop at entry of $523 — a stated -6.7% maximum loss. The position was held to -51.2%. The lesson: hard stops must be enforced mechanically at the time the price is breached, regardless of thesis conviction, OBV signals, or 'it will come back' reasoning. For every position, set a calendar reminder or price alert. If the stock is below the stop at the weekly review, the default action is EXIT unless there is an explicit, documented reason to override. Trigger: any position below its stated stop price at weekly review — close it unless there is documented thesis-strengthening news from that week.

## Lesson 10 [5]
When the broad market shows MACD bullish signals across 70%+ of tracked stocks simultaneously, this is a market-wide technical bounce, not stock-specific strength. In these relief rally environments, MACD alone is an unreliable entry signal. Raise the bar for new long entries to require MACD bullish AND OBV rising AND above SMA50 — all three together. Similarly, do not exit shorts based on MACD turning bullish alone during broad relief rallies if OBV remains flat/falling and price is below SMA50. Trigger: scan the MACD column at each weekly review — if more than 70% of names are MACD bullish, apply the elevated three-signal standard.

## Lesson 11 [2]
When holding a short position within 5% of its stop price heading into a scheduled earnings event, the gap risk must be explicitly assessed. If the earnings report could plausibly show turnaround progress (as with SBUX Q2), the position is inadequately protected — the stock can gap through the stop before it can be executed. Action: when a short is within 5% of its stop and earnings are within 7 days, either (1) tighten the stop to create more buffer on the other side, or (2) reduce position size by 50% pre-earnings and restore it post-event if the thesis remains intact. Trigger: any short position within 5% of its stop price when a known earnings date falls within the next 7 days.

## Lesson 12 [2]
When a sector-specialist analyst (e.g., Jefferies on solar, Goldman on retail) issues a downgrade to Hold/Sell with a price target within 5% of the current market price, treat it as a thesis-completion signal — not a minor event. It means the investment thesis has become market consensus and the asymmetric upside is gone. This is an exit signal even if the fundamental narrative remains structurally valid. The market has priced in the thesis. Trigger: any position receiving a Hold/Sell downgrade with PT within 5% of current price from a sector-relevant research house — initiate exit.

---

## Lesson 14 [3]
When the primary macro driver of a thematic position reverses materially (e.g., tariff shock theme invalidated by actual trade deal with significant reduction), immediately re-evaluate ALL positions entered on that macro driver. The test: 'Does this thesis survive without the macro tailwind?' Names with strong individual fundamentals (pricing power, secular demand, superior unit economics) survive the theme weakening; pure-macro plays without independent thesis legs should be exited. Trigger: any confirmed trade deal, policy reversal, or macro regime change that directly addresses the original thesis catalyst — run through every position within 24 hours and explicitly answer whether the individual thesis survives the new regime.

---

## Lesson 15 [4]
When a high-profile AI founder or CEO with direct infrastructure spending authority makes a named, public commitment to specific GPU suppliers (e.g., Musk on CNBC: 'we expect to still buy a lot of GPUs from NVIDIA and AMD'), treat it as an asymmetric near-term bullish catalyst more reliable than analyst upgrades — this is a named buyer committing real capital, not a forecast. It should strengthen the thesis notes of all affected positions and trigger evaluation of whether to add to those positions if conviction is elevated. Trigger: any AI infrastructure decision-maker at a major hyperscaler or AI lab naming specific hardware suppliers in a major public forum — immediately assess impact on NVDA, AMD, AVGO, TSM, and ARM positions.

---

## Lesson 16 [2]
When a SHORT position's technical entry signals materially reverse — specifically when MACD turns bullish AND price crosses above SMA50 — exit the short regardless of whether the fundamental thesis remains intact. Technical reversals signal institutional covering that can run well ahead of any fundamental deterioration. The original short entry required specific technical conditions; when those conditions invert, the edge is gone and holding becomes speculation. Trigger: any short position where 2+ of the 4 original entry technical signals have reversed — exit or reduce by 50% and tighten stop to within 3% of current price. (Lesson validated by CRM short: AI disruption thesis intact, but technical setup fully reversed from MACD bearish/below SMA50 to MACD bullish/above SMA50.)
