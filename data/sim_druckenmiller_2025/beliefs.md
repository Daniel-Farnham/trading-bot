# Investment Beliefs

## Policy catalyst framework: demand vs. supply, before vs. after [3]
Known policy catalysts with clear directional outcomes are among the highest-conviction setups — size up BEFORE the catalyst fires, not after. Apply a three-part test: (1) DEMAND or SUPPLY? Demand-side catalysts (LNG export approvals creating new revenue streams) are unambiguously bullish for producers. Supply-side catalysts (drill more to lower domestic prices) are paradoxically bearish. (2) SPECULATIVE or FUNDAMENTAL? For momentum assets, even neutral outcomes disappoint when maximum bullish is priced in. (3) TARIFF EXPOSURE: In any tariff escalation environment, immediately classify each position for trade sensitivity. Healthcare with domestic FDA-approved demand is tariff-immune and should be sized up before escalation reaches consensus. AI infrastructure, globally-exposed cyclicals, and financial sector names face tariff-driven multiple compression regardless of fundamental thesis quality. Pre-catalyst sizing discipline applies both to policy catalysts and to tariff regime shifts — the right time to own tariff-immune assets is before the trade war escalates.

## Institutional Flow as Primary Signal [3]
OBV and synchronized sector rotation signals override narrative thesis quality in the short to medium term. When institutions move money, they move in bulk — OBV captures this before price confirms it. Core rules: (1) OBV rising during price selloff = institutional accumulation, do not exit regardless of price pain. (2) OBV falling during price rise = distribution, begin reducing. (3) Synchronized OBV movement across a sector (Lesson 2 + Lesson 8) = institutional rotation in progress, follow the flow. (4) OBV reversal on a pyramided position = reduce to pre-pyramid size immediately (Lesson 10). The thesis explains WHY to own something; OBV tells you WHETHER institutions agree right now. When thesis and OBV diverge, trust OBV for near-term sizing decisions while keeping the thesis for the core position floor.
