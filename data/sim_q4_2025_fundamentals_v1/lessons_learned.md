# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [1]
On first deployment, resist the urge to fill the portfolio immediately. 3 positions at ~26% deployed preserves optionality for better entries and regime confirmation over subsequent weeks.

---

## Lesson 2 [3]
When all three of RSI momentum, MACD trend, and OBV institutional flow align bullishly — that is the highest-quality entry signal. Require all three before opening a position, not just one or two.

---

## Lesson 3 [5]
Avoid shorting names already at RSI < 30 unless ADX > 35 confirms a strong, sustained downtrend. RSI=22 (CMCSA) or RSI=24 (PARA) may bounce before continuing lower — wait for the dead-cat rally to short into.

---

## Lesson 4 [2]
Prioritize entry timing within theme: if LLY and REGN are both valid healthcare plays, choose the one with lower RSI and not near upper Bollinger Band. RSI=59 (REGN) is a better entry than RSI=69 (LLY) when fundamentals are comparably strong.

---

## Lesson 5 [5]
When MACD turns bearish but OBV remains rising, institutional accumulation is overriding short-term momentum deterioration. This is a 'buy the dip' signal, not an exit signal — especially when ADX confirms a strong underlying trend. Do not exit positions on MACD bearish alone if OBV is rising.

---

## Lesson 6 [5]
When a short position's MACD turns bullish AND RSI climbs above 50 AND ADX declines below 30, all three entry conditions have simultaneously deteriorated — this is a systematic exit signal. Do not anchor to the original entry thesis when the technical evidence that justified it has reversed. Cover the short and take the loss. The cost of holding a technically-broken short (unlimited upside exposure) far exceeds the cost of re-entering later if conditions reassert.

---

## Lesson 7 [1]
Lesson 7: When a short position's sector peer shows extreme strength (RSI>65, ADX>40, MACD bullish), treat it as an early warning signal to exit the short even before Lesson 6's full three-condition trigger is formally met. Sector rotation is real — a surging industrial sector (CAT at RSI=71, ADX=46) undermines a short in the same sector (DE) regardless of individual company fundamentals. The peer's strength is forward-looking information the single-stock technicals haven't yet priced in.

---

## Lesson 8 [5]
Lesson 8: When OBV falls AND MACD turns bearish simultaneously on a LONG position, consider discretionary early exit (before the full triple-signal SMA50 breach) when ALL of the following apply: (1) position is at a loss or minimal gain, (2) a known fundamental weakness exists (thin margins, accounting risk, execution history concerns), and (3) ADX is below 25 (no strong institutional trend to sustain a recovery). The triple-signal rule is the hard, systematic exit. This discretionary early-exit rule prevents small -3% losses from becoming -15%+ drawdowns on structurally vulnerable positions when two of three distribution signals are already confirmed.

## Lesson 9 [2]
Lesson 9: When a SHORT target announces M&A exploration or banker retention, evaluate deal quality before deciding to cover. Companies in structural decline that pursue acquisitions of other declining assets typically: (1) increase leverage on an already deteriorating revenue base, (2) signal management has exhausted organic growth options, and (3) create integration complexity that accelerates value destruction. Treat M&A by a declining company as thesis confirmation unless the acquisition target is genuinely high-growth and the acquisition price would force a cover above your stop. The tell: healthy companies acquire growing assets; structurally declining companies acquire other declining assets to hide the decay. Trigger condition: short target retains bankers — first assess whether the potential deal is growth-accretive or decline-consolidating before acting.

## Lesson 10 [2]
Lesson 10: When a LONG position reaches within 5% of its stated target price AND RSI exceeds 75 AND receives an analyst downgrade to Hold or Sell, treat these three conditions together as a signal to either (a) take partial profits immediately or (b) extend the target only if MACD remains bullish AND OBV remains rising AND you can articulate a new, specific fundamental catalyst beyond the original thesis. The original target price was set when the stock was cheaper — at RSI=80 near the target, the risk/reward has fundamentally shifted. The asymmetry is now to the downside. Trigger conditions: within 5% of target AND RSI>75 AND analyst downgrade received.

## Lesson 11 [3]
Lesson 11: When a SHORT position's gain has eroded to near-breakeven AND 2 of 3 Lesson 6 exit conditions are met AND ADX has declined significantly from its peak (e.g., from 42 toward 34) AND OBV has shifted from falling to flat, take the remaining gain and exit rather than waiting for the strict 3/3 trigger. On a short position, holding through a technical deterioration with minimal profit creates asymmetric downside — unlimited upside exposure for a position already showing 2 of 3 exit signals. The T short demonstrated this exactly: MACD bullish + RSI crossing 50 + ADX declining + OBV turning flat = deteriorating setup with no reward for continued patience. Trigger conditions: 2/3 Lesson 6 conditions confirmed AND ADX declining from a prior peak above 40 AND OBV transitioned from falling to flat AND remaining gain is below 2%.

## Lesson 12 [2]
Lesson 12: When multiple hyperscalers announce large-scale AI infrastructure commitments in the same reporting period (e.g., Google $40B + Microsoft billions in the same week), treat this as a hold signal — not a 'sell the news' trigger — for AI infrastructure longs where OBV is rising. Simultaneous multi-company capex confirms the buildout is in early-to-middle innings, not near the peak. The correct response is to check OBV across core AI holdings: if OBV is rising despite price weakness, institutions are accumulating into the capex news, confirming continued upside. Trigger conditions: 2+ hyperscalers announce >$5B AI capex commitments in the same week AND OBV is rising across the core AI position basket.

## Lesson 13 [5]
Lesson 13: When a long position has EXCEEDED its original target price AND RSI > 80 AND MACD remains bullish AND OBV remains rising AND ADX > 35, do NOT exit mechanically at the original target — extend the target 15-20% above the breached level and simultaneously raise the stop to lock in the majority of gains. The original target was set when the position was cheaper and the institutional picture was less clear; at RSI > 80 with ADX > 35 (confirmed institutional conviction), MACD bullish (trend strong), and OBV rising (accumulation continuing), the technical evidence argues that the move has further to run. Exiting mechanically at a price target set months earlier abandons a position with the strongest possible institutional backing. Trigger conditions: current price > original target AND RSI > 80 AND MACD bullish AND OBV rising AND ADX > 35.

## Lesson 14 [1]
Lesson 14: When a thesis position's primary competitive moat (e.g., GPU monopoly) is publicly challenged by key customers exploring alternatives at scale (e.g., hyperscalers testing competing AI chips), treat this as a 'thesis moderation event' — not invalidation. If this customer-level diversification news coincides with a triple distribution technical signal (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling), treat the combination as a high-conviction exit signal even when the long-term thesis narrative remains intellectually compelling. A thesis that is 'still right long-term but softening near-term' with deteriorating technicals is not a reason to hold — it is a reason to exit and re-enter when both the technicals reset AND the moat narrative re-strengthens. Trigger conditions: customer-level announcements of alternative vendor exploration at material scale + triple distribution technical signal present simultaneously.

---

## Lesson 15 [1]
Lesson 16: When a LONG position's stated stop price is breached by current market price AND the position is at a loss exceeding 10%, EXIT with discipline even when the company's fundamentals have materially improved since entry. Improving fundamentals on a stop-breached position typically indicates the original entry was timed too aggressively into a momentum peak — the stock priced in the good news before the entry, and price action has since confirmed that read. Improved fundamentals are a reason to RE-ENTER after technical reset, not a reason to override the stop. The stop exists to bound downside; holding through it while waiting for fundamentals to re-rate the stock creates indefinite loss exposure with no defined exit. Trigger conditions: current price < stated stop price AND position at a loss greater than 10%.
