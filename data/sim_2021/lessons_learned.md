## Lesson 2
First deployment week: resist the urge to deploy aggressively. The 60% cash floor exists because the macro regime needs validation. Deploy into the 2-3 highest conviction ideas only, then let the thesis prove itself before adding more risk.

## Lesson 4
Intel CEO changes are a high-signal event for the semiconductor ecosystem: when the dominant incumbent makes a desperate strategic move like replacing leadership, it confirms the competitive moat has eroded. This is the moment to increase exposure to the challengers (TSMC, AVGO, AMD) — not wait for the new CEO's plan. The incumbents' crisis is the challengers' durable tailwind.

## Lesson 5
When a position is showing a mild technical deterioration (NVDA below SMA50) but the fundamental thesis is intact and sector news is confirming demand, the correct response is to hold — not cut. Differentiate between thesis invalidation and short-term price noise.

## Lesson 6
RSI=83 into an earnings binary event is a position management signal: do not add, and consider a partial trim to reduce binary risk. A correct thesis does not immunize against a 10-15% earnings-driven drawdown. The rule: RSI > 80 + imminent catalyst = hold existing size only, do not add, and set a mental stop on earnings disappointment rather than the wide catastrophic stop.

## Lesson 7
When a short position receives multiple high-profile institutional upgrades (e.g., Morgan Stanley 'Ready for Takeoff' on BA) and bullish media amplification in the same week, the short squeeze risk increases materially even if the fundamental thesis is unchanged. The correct response is NOT to immediately cover — the thesis must be evaluated independently — but to tighten the mental stop closer to the entry price and reduce the required conviction threshold for exit. Upgrades generate retail buying momentum that can temporarily disconnect price from fundamentals.

## Lesson 12

## Lesson 13
The reflation/rising rate rotation creates a bifurcated portfolio impact: growth/long-duration names (high P/E tech, utilities, REITs) face multiple compression as the discount rate rises, while financials (margin expansion), energy (demand recovery), and cyclicals (pricing power) benefit simultaneously. When Lesson 12 triggers (simultaneous MACD bearish flips across growth names), the active response is to ADD rate-beneficiary positions (banks, energy, industrials) to offset the headwind — not just hold growth and wait for the rotation to exhaust. Hedging the macro factor actively is superior to passive exposure.

## Lesson 14
Short position exit discipline: When a short develops THREE simultaneous adverse signals — (1) MACD flips bullish, (2) RSI moves above 55 and price above SMA50, AND (3) positive fundamental news creates a narrative tailwind — exit the position regardless of whether the fundamental thesis is still partially valid. Fundamental theses play out over quarters; price action follows technicals and news momentum over weeks. A short that is technically broken and receiving positive catalysts will continue to work against you before it eventually resolves in your favor. The asymmetric risk of holding a technically-broken short is not worth the wait.

## Lesson 15
Shorting high-dividend legacy businesses (T at ~7% yield) during early-stage reflation rotations carries asymmetric risk beyond what fundamentals suggest. As 10-year yields rise and growth stocks fall, value-oriented investors rotate into high-yield 'cheap' stocks as yield alternatives — temporarily pushing prices against the short even when the structural decline thesis is unchanged. In rising-rate rotation environments, yield-stock shorts face a counterintuitive buyer (value rotators) that does not exist in purely momentum-driven markets. Rule: In a confirmed reflation rotation (10-year rising >50bps in 6 weeks), short yield-legacy stocks at maximum 4% allocation with RSI>55 as the exit trigger rather than the standard RSI>60 used for growth shorts — the margin of safety must be tighter because the rate tailwind for the short thesis is partially offset by the value rotation inflow.

## Lesson 16
When portfolio cash falls below the 20% minimum floor, the correct action is NOT to sell winners — it is to trim the largest unrealized loss that still has an intact thesis. Selling winners to raise cash sacrifices upside from working positions; trimming losers maintains quality while restoring capital discipline. Execute the trim at normal market conditions, not in panic. Rule: cash below 20% floor = immediately trim the worst-performing position with intact thesis by enough to restore the floor, regardless of MACD or RSI state.

## Lesson 18
Ledger vs. independent market data discrepancy: When a position's recorded price and P&L in the internal ledger (e.g., TMO at $301.34, -41.9%) diverges significantly from independent technical data (TMO at $452.01), treat the independent market data as authoritative for investment decisions. Internal ledger entries can compound errors over time. A position that appears to be at catastrophic stop territory may actually be within normal drawdown — and premature exits based on corrupted ledger data destroy value. Always cross-reference ledger prices against independent data before making stop-loss or close decisions.

## Lesson 24
Cash floor sequencing: When portfolio cash is at or below 22% (within 2% of the 20% floor), the mandatory sequence before any new long is: (1) identify and close the weakest existing position, (2) confirm cash restoration above 25%, (3) then open the new position. Opening a new position before closing the offset position risks breaching the 20% floor if markets move intraday. The rule is close-first, then buy — never simultaneous.

## Lesson 31

Rule: When financials develop all three adverse signals concurrent with a Fed hawkish pivot signal, treat as thesis invalidation rather than oversold bounce opportunity. They are distinct trades that can diverge.

When a utility policy-catalyst position shows 2+ adverse technical signals in a growth bull market with -5%+ P&L, close it and redeploy into an operating-leverage expression of the same theme. ## Lesson 38
Lesson 38 — Risk-off yield decline bifurcates portfolio along growth vs reflation lines: When Delta variant or macro risk-off fears drive the 10-year Treasury yield sharply lower, the portfolio impact is non-uniform and predictable. Growth and long-duration tech longs with high P/E multiples BENEFIT from the lower discount rate — the same fear that causes MACD bearish signals across growth names is simultaneously reducing the discount rate that supports those multiples. Commodity and reflation trades SUFFER as the growth-scare narrative undermines demand expectations — FCX short, energy names, and industrials all face headwinds simultaneously. Both legs can be captured: hold growth longs through MACD bearish signals in risk-off episodes and maintain commodity shorts that are technically confirmed. The mistake is treating risk-off as universally negative — it is only negative for rate-beneficiary financial names and commodity/cyclical longs. Rule: When 10-year yield falls >20bps on macro fear in a single week, do NOT close growth tech longs on MACD bearish signals alone — the lower discount rate is actively supporting those multiples and the MACD weakness is macro-driven rotation noise rather than thesis deterioration. Instead, use the environment to confirm or add short exposure in commodity and reflation names showing simultaneous technical deterioration.
