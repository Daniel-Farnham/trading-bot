# Investment Beliefs

## Overseas Manufacturing Is a Structural Short in Tariff Regimes [3]
When a sustained tariff regime targets manufacturing countries (Vietnam, Indonesia, China), companies with heavy production exposure in those regions face dual compression: input cost increases that erode margin if absorbed, or demand destruction if passed through to consumers. This materializes in actual earnings deterioration over 1-3 quarters. Entry screen: heavy Asia-Pacific manufacturing + consumer discretionary or brand-dependent revenue + falling/flat OBV + thin margins (<10%). The edge is entering before earnings confirmation, using OBV distribution as the early signal. NKE (+12.6% profit) and SBUX (Q2 confirmed) are the canonical validation trades. The thesis is NOT invalidated by a tariff pause on non-relevant countries — only direct relief on the specific manufacturing country matters.

## Hold High-ADX Shorts Through Policy-Driven Bounces [3]
When a strong-trending short position (ADX > 35 at entry) experiences a 1-3 day bounce driven by a policy or earnings catalyst, the correct response is thesis validation, not price-based exit. Checklist: (1) Is the bounce directly relevant to THIS company's specific thesis driver? If no, it is noise. (2) Is OBV still flat or falling? If yes, no institutional reversal is occurring. (3) Is ADX still elevated? If yes, primary trend intact. Capitulating on high-conviction shorts during bounces is the primary error in bear market execution — the short-covering itself is evidence the trend is intact. SBUX ADX=39 through Q2 bounce is the canonical example.
