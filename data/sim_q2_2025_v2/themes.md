# Investment Themes

## Tariff Shock & Trade War Escalation [1]
Trump's Liberation Day tariffs are creating structural disruption to global supply chains. Companies with heavy import exposure (tech hardware, consumer goods, semiconductors) face margin compression. This is not a short-term trade negotiation — it is a sustained regime shift favoring domestic production.

---

## Defensive Domestic Rotation [1]
Capital is rotating from globally-exposed growth stocks into domestic-facing defensives: US energy, managed healthcare, discount retail, and telecom. These businesses are largely insulated from tariff exposure and benefit from a risk-off macro environment.

---

## AI/Semiconductor Under Pressure [4]
The AI infrastructure trade faces a dual headwind: tariffs disrupting semiconductor supply chains (especially Taiwan/TSMC), and slowing enterprise capex as corporate uncertainty rises. NVDA, AVGO, TSM, ARM all show bearish technicals with OBV falling.

---

## US Energy Independence [2]
Domestic US energy producers (XOM, CVX, LNG) are benefiting from the energy security narrative and relative advantage vs. internationally-sourced energy. Tariffs create a moat for domestic producers. Oil majors with low leverage and high cash generation are outperforming.

---

## Domestic Manufacturing Advantage [1]
Tariffs create automatic competitive moats for US-based manufacturers competing against now-tariffed imports. FSLR vs. Chinese solar, domestic energy vs. imported LNG, US steel vs. imported steel. These companies gain pricing power without changing their business — the tariff does the work for them.

---

## Nuclear & Data Center Power [4]
Hyperscaler AI power demand is creating structural secular demand for 24/7 clean baseload power that only nuclear can provide at scale. Microsoft, Google, and Amazon are signing 20-year nuclear PPAs as AI inference workloads require constant, reliable power that wind/solar cannot deliver. CEG and VST are diverging positively from broader energy sector weakness (XOM, CVX, OXY all below SMA50) — institutional accumulation is pricing in contracted clean power demand ahead of broader recognition.
