# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [3]
When a binary political event creates a clear policy regime change (e.g., Senate flips), act on the highest-conviction policy beneficiaries immediately — the market prices this within days, not weeks. Hesitating costs significant alpha on event-driven thesis entries.

## Lesson 2 [5]
ADX > 45 with OBV rising is a 'strong trend with institutional backing' signal — do NOT fade it on RSI alone. Extended RSI in a high-ADX environment means trend continuation, not reversal. Size appropriately but enter.

---

## Lesson 3 [2]
When a company beats earnings AND raises guidance while in a strong technical uptrend (ADX>50, OBV rising), this is an 'add on confirmation' signal — not a take-profit signal. Fundamental validation of a thesis in motion justifies increasing position size, not trimming.

---

## Lesson 4 [2]
ADX>70 is a rare, high-conviction institutional trend signal. When combined with OBV rising and MACD bullish, it indicates a structural trend already in motion — not speculation. The appropriate response is to size into the trend, not wait for a pullback that may not come. Use medium confidence sizing (5-8%) due to elevated volatility typical at these ADX levels.

## Lesson 5 [3]
Retail-coordinated short squeezes (WallStreetBets model) can rapidly create idiosyncratic political risk for financial sector holdings even when the macro thesis is unchanged. When this happens, differentiate within the sector: exit pure capital markets names first (GS — most exposed to regulatory backlash against short selling and trading practices) before diversified consumer banks (JPM — yield curve and consumer lending thesis less threatened). The specific business model exposure matters more than the sector label.

## Lesson 6 [5]
Rising 10-year yields create a systematic rotation away from high-multiple long-duration assets (clean energy, growth software, high-ADX semiconductors) toward financials, commodity producers, and value cyclicals. The technical fingerprint of this rotation appears BEFORE fundamental deterioration: OBV begins falling + MACD turns bearish in growth names while OBV rises in financials/value names. Trigger: When 10-year yield rises >50bps in a month, proactively review all high-multiple positions for this technical signature and be willing to exit even if fundamental thesis is intact. The correct response is to rotate, not hold through rate compression.

## Lesson 7 [5]
Supply chain disruptions create DIVERGENT signals across a portfolio depending on a company's position in the value chain: a chip shortage is simultaneously a BULLISH fundamental catalyst for semiconductor manufacturers (MU — pricing power, demand confirmation, supply constraint validation) and a BEARISH near-term headwind for chip consumers (F — production cuts, lost revenue, margin pressure). When a supply constraint news event hits, always evaluate impact separately for each portfolio holding based on whether they sit upstream (supplier) or downstream (consumer) in that supply chain. Trigger: When major supply shortage news breaks for any commodity or component.

## Lesson 8 [1]
When a major legacy incumbent announces a credible acceleration into a disrupted sector where you hold a 'first mover advantage' thesis (e.g., Toyota announcing aggressive EV plans while holding Ford as the EV pioneer), reassess whether the first-mover moat is durable. If the competitive advantage was predicated on early leadership before incumbents committed — and incumbents are now committing at scale — the thesis requires revision even if the structural transition remains valid. The correct action is to evaluate whether your holding's differentiation holds against scaled incumbent competition. Trigger: Major industry incumbent announces specific, funded, credible acceleration into a disruptive sector you hold exposure to.

## Lesson 9 [1]
Re-entry thesis time-validity window: When re-entering a position based on a specific technical reversal signal (OBV reversal from falling to rising + ADX recovering), set a 4-week validity window for that accumulation signal to hold. If OBV resumes falling or ADX collapses within this window, the re-entry thesis was false — exit immediately without waiting for further confirmation. The false re-entry risk is compounded by psychological anchoring to the original exit rationale. Trigger: Any position where a re-entry was made specifically on technical accumulation reversal, and the accumulation signal reverses within 4 weeks of entry.

## Lesson 10 [2]
Lesson 10: Post-entry ADX collapse as thesis-evaporation exit signal — when a position is entered on ADX > 35 institutional accumulation, monitor ADX trajectory post-entry. If ADX collapses below 20 within 1-2 months AND OBV simultaneously reverses to falling, the institutional trend that justified entry has evaporated. Exit promptly without waiting for the full Lesson 2 exit threshold (ADX > 30 + OBV falling), because collapsed ADX means the trend is already over — there is no active distribution to measure. The trap is holding because MACD remains bullish or RSI is moderate; these are oscillator/momentum signals that lag the trend signal. The trend confirmation (ADX) has already spoken. Trigger: Any position entered on ADX > 35 where ADX falls below 20 post-entry and OBV reverses to falling within a 6-week window.
