# Investment Beliefs

## Thesis Integrity Over Price Action [3]
When a position is in drawdown OR has exceeded its price target, the exit decision must be driven by institutional momentum signals — not price movement alone. Three-sided principle: (1) HOLD through drawdowns when ADX >25 with OBV rising and no fundamental thesis change — price weakness is noise, not signal; (2) HOLD and RAISE TARGET through target breaches when ADX is flat/rising from entry and OBV is rising — the market is confirming the thesis has more runway than originally modeled; (3) EXIT when: thesis breaks on fundamentals, OR ADX declines >20 points from entry AND MACD turns bearish AND price is at/above target (Lesson 6), OR ADX declines >30 points AND falls below 18 AND OBV falls pre-target (Lesson 7). The unifying principle: price alone is never the exit trigger — institutional momentum signals are. NVDA exemplifies principle #2: $650 target breached, ADX=41 stable from entry, Wells Fargo raising to $875.
