# Lessons Learned

## Lesson 1 [2]
Political catalyst trades require immediate action: when a binary political event resolves (election, Senate runoff), the resulting policy tailwinds are real and multi-year. Waiting for RSI to cool on the initial move risks missing the structural re-rating. Enter on the catalyst, not on a pullback that may never come.

---

## Lesson 2 [1]
First deployment should be concentrated in 3 highest-conviction ideas: spreading too thin across 8-10 positions in the first review adds complexity without proportional conviction. 3 positions at 6-7% each with 80%+ cash gives optionality for future reviews when more data confirms or denies themes.

---

## Lesson 3 [5]
When a position is down 5-10% but the structural thesis is intact AND technicals show strong ADX (>35) with OBV rising, the correct action is to hold — not exit. Price weakness in the context of institutional accumulation (rising OBV) and a confirmed trend (high ADX) is noise, not signal. Only exit when the thesis is broken, not when the price is temporarily lower.

---

## Lesson 4 [1]
Short squeeze risk in heavily-shorted stocks is violent and existential for short positions: the GME/WallStreetBets event demonstrates that coordinated retail buying can force institutional short covers at catastrophic prices regardless of fundamental thesis. Before opening any short: verify (1) short float is not >15-20%, (2) no unusual options open interest suggesting gamma squeeze setup, (3) no active retail/social media cult following. Our VZ short is in a large-cap with diverse institutional holders — safe. Never short small/mid-cap stocks with high retail ownership and elevated short float.

## Lesson 5 [1]
Hedge fund blow-ups (Archegos, LTCM-type events) create indiscriminate forced selling in stocks held by the blowing-up entity — this looks like thesis breaks but is market structure noise. The tell is when high-quality names in confirmed uptrends sell off without corresponding fundamental deterioration. Counterintuitively, quality banks that managed exposure well (GS exiting Archegos early) are BUY signals on the dip, not sells. Trigger: when headlines reference hedge fund margin calls and your longs sell off — verify whether your holdings were actually in the blow-up portfolio before treating it as thesis invalidation.

## Lesson 6 [3]
Take profits when a position simultaneously hits its price target AND its primary trend indicator (ADX) has declined significantly from entry. DE exemplifies this: price exceeded the $365 target at $375.60 while ADX dropped from 46 to 22 and MACD turned bearish. The thesis can remain intact — the position has simply delivered its projected return with declining momentum. Trigger: price at or above target + ADX declined >20 points from entry level + MACD bearish = disciplined exit regardless of fundamental conviction. Do not let a winning trade become a round-trip by ignoring technical deterioration at target.

---

## Lesson 7 [1]
When ADX collapses more than 30 points from a strong entry level (>40) and falls below 18, AND OBV is simultaneously falling, the institutional momentum that justified the position has dissipated — exit even if the fundamental thesis remains intact and price has not hit target. The thesis may still be correct but the timing has extended indefinitely; capital earns better returns in positions where institutional trend is confirmed. Trigger: ADX decline >30 points from entry + ADX now below 18 + OBV falling + price more than 15% below target. This is distinct from Lesson 6 (price at target + ADX decline = take profits) — it applies specifically to pre-target exits on momentum evaporation.

---

## Lesson 8 [1]
When a high-conviction value investor (Buffett, Druckenmiller-tier) is publicly disclosed as entering the long side of your short position, treat it as an immediate exit trigger regardless of whether the fundamental short thesis has changed. These investors attract institutional momentum buyers that can create multi-month re-rating moves orthogonal to fundamentals. The correct action is to cover immediately and re-evaluate from the sidelines — not to fight the Buffett bid. Trigger: any news confirming a top-tier value institution is long in a name we are short = cover within the same review cycle.
