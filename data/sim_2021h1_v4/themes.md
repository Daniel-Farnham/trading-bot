# Investment Themes

## Blue Wave Fiscal Stimulus [4]
Democrats control all three branches of government after Georgia Senate runoffs. $1.4T+ stimulus packages, infrastructure spending, and social programs are now politically executable. This structurally benefits banks (higher rates), materials/industrials (infrastructure), clean energy (policy mandates), and consumer spending (direct checks).

---

## Clean Energy Transition [4]
Biden's climate agenda — ITC extensions, EV mandates, utility-scale renewable procurement — is now legislatively viable. Solar, wind, and energy storage companies will benefit from policy tailwinds for 4+ years. ENPH, NEE, FSLR are core plays.

---

## Semiconductor Infrastructure [5]
Global chip shortage is structural — AI, 5G, automotive electrification, and cloud compute are all chip-intensive and growing simultaneously. TSMC, NVDA, and the semiconductor supply chain will be capacity-constrained for 2-3 years, driving pricing power and margin expansion.

---

## Financial Re-rating [3]
Banks and brokerages are being re-rated higher on: (1) Blue Wave stimulus = higher long-term rates = better net interest margins, (2) fiscal spending = more deal flow for investment banks, (3) economic reopening = credit loss reserves can be released. GS, JPM, SCHW are core plays.

---

## Healthcare Aging [5]
The aging U.S. population creates a 20-year structural tailwind for healthcare companies — particularly managed care (UNH), medical devices (ISRG), and pharmaceutical innovation (LLY, NVO). Medicare Advantage enrollment grows 8-10% annually as 10,000 Boomers turn 65 every day. Biden's ACA expansion adds incremental coverage. Healthcare is uniquely defensive (non-cyclical demand) while also having growth optionality through technology-enabled care delivery. This theme is sector-specific and separate from clean energy or AI — it deserves explicit tracking.
