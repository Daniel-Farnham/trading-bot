# Seed Beliefs

Cross-regime investment principles consolidated from multiple simulation runs.
These are loaded for live trading only — never during backtests.

## Hard Stop Discipline [regimes: 8]
Hard stop prices and thesis-pillar invalidations are mandatory exit triggers — not suggestions subject to override by conviction or indicators. Before treating any distribution signal as a full exit trigger, apply the triage question: 'Does this specific event directly impair this company's core revenue or value driver (>20% of earnings or the exact stated thesis condition)?' For macro-driven triple distribution, apply a second triage question: 'Does this recession or policy shock break this specific thesis?' — if NO (domestic revenue, pricing power, non-cyclical demand), hold through distribution and treat OBV rising as the accumulation signal; if YES (rate-sensitive, cyclical, global supply chain exposed), exit per standard triple distribution discipline. Pre-program exit criteria before positions are under pressure; one exception applies when an earnings catalyst is within 14 days and the stock remains above SMA50 — suspend evaluation until post-earnings, as institutional hedging ahead of a catalyst is not distribution.

## Disciplined Capital Deployment [regimes: 9]
Never fully deploy capital during the first review of a new macro regime, after a major policy shock, or when entering a new period without prior market context — open scouts only (1-2 positions, 5% each) to test regime conviction before committing core capital, maintaining a 40%+ cash buffer early. Upgrade from scout to core only when three conditions align simultaneously: position up 5%+, OBV rising, and an independent external signal confirms the thesis (analyst upgrade, earnings beat, institutional options flow, major partnership) — never upgrade on price action alone. When a catalyst has already driven 8%+ gains and is imminent or has just been implemented, the market has front-run the announcement — do not add capital at this stage; a new independent fundamental catalyst is required to re-accelerate, and technicals will typically fade (MACD bearish, OBV flat) as the front-run trade unwinds.

## OBV as Primary Institutional Signal [regimes: 9]
OBV is the authoritative signal for institutional behavior and overrides RSI, price momentum, and analyst narrative as an entry, exit, and re-entry trigger in any regime: rising OBV means accumulation regardless of overbought RSI or negative headlines, and 'good news + falling OBV' is more dangerous than 'bad news + falling OBV' because institutions use positive catalysts as liquidity to distribute. When OBV reverses on a position that was pyramided citing OBV rising as the primary rationale, the pyramid increment is immediately invalidated — reduce back to pre-pyramid core allocation while retaining fundamental thesis exposure, because 'the business is still good fundamentally' is not equivalent to 'the reason we added capital at this specific price still applies.' For position upgrades, apply a two-condition threshold before acting on a single OBV reversal: OBV must be falling for 2+ consecutive reviews AND MACD must turn bearish; weight OBV signals during thin-volume periods less heavily than sustained signals in normal-volume periods.

## Technical Confluence for Entry, Exit, and Target Management [regimes: 9]
No single technical signal is sufficient for entry, exit, or target decisions in any regime — require confluence of RSI momentum, MACD trend, and OBV institutional flow, all three aligned. For triple distribution exits (below SMA50 + MACD bearish + OBV falling), triage cause before acting: macro-driven distribution in thesis-intact, non-cyclical positions should be held and potentially added to on OBV rising divergence (price-OBV divergence during a broad selloff is the primary accumulation signal); thesis-driven or macro-sensitive distribution requires exit without delay — waiting 'one more week' on a confirmed triple distribution signal costs real capital with low probability of reversal. For shorts, only cover when MACD is bullish AND OBV is rising simultaneously; when two of three distribution signals are active, pre-program the unconditional exit trigger for the third signal rather than leaving the decision to in-the-moment judgment under pressure.

## Government Policy as Binary Thesis Events [regimes: 3]
Government actions — tariffs, export controls, regulatory rulings, price controls, or major platform partner decisions — require immediate same-review-cycle thesis evaluation, classifying all holdings into: (A) thesis directly premised on the policy that changed, requiring immediate review and potential exit; (B) policy as a tailwind but not the primary driver, hold if primary drivers intact; (C) policy-agnostic, no action required. Critically distinguish permanence: export controls targeting specific architectures on national security grounds are structurally durable and should be treated as permanent revenue impairment (not a negotiable tariff and not recoverable via redesign), while tariff-based actions can be reversed; platform distribution concentration (single partner controlling >50% of revenue access) carries equivalent structural permanence and is invisible to standard tariff-triage analysis. OBV remains the arbiter throughout — policy-driven price dislocations with OBV rising are entry opportunities, not thesis threats; OBV falling on or before the announcement is the exit signal, not the policy text itself.
