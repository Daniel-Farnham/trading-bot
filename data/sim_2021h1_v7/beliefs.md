# Investment Beliefs

## Macro Regime Shifts Create Predictable Sector Rotations [3]
Major macro catalysts — political trifectas, rapid yield cycle inflections, large-scale fiscal programs — create mechanical and directional sector rotations that are identifiable in advance and act within defined timeframes. Political regime changes (Senate flip, presidential transition) immediately activate the new government's policy priorities as investable themes: clean energy, infrastructure, fiscal-sensitive sectors move first and fast within 1-2 weeks. Rapid Treasury yield increases (>40bps over 4-6 weeks) mechanically compress high-PE growth multiples and trigger institutional rotation into energy, banks, and materials — regardless of whether the underlying growth thesis remains valid. These rotations are predictable precisely because they are structural and institutional: fund mandates, risk models, and macro overlays force the rotation. Position ahead of the catalyst, not after the narrative saturates. Once a rotation is widely discussed in financial media, the easy money has been made.

## Institutional Positioning Is Readable Through Technical Flows [3]
Institutional accumulation and distribution are visible in real time through a three-factor technical framework before price confirms the move. Accumulation signal (entry): ADX >40 + MACD bullish + OBV rising simultaneously — institutions are building positions; this is the highest-probability entry signal we have identified. Distribution signal (exit): MACD bearish + OBV falling simultaneously, even while above SMA50 — this is the WARNING stage; institutions are exiting before price breaks. Below SMA50 is CONFIRMATION, not the first signal. The critical distinction between a dip and a breakdown: OBV rising during a pullback means institutions are buying (hold); OBV falling during a pullback means institutions are exiting (sell within 1-2 cycles). This framework has been confirmed across six position entries (NUE, UNH, HD, ISRG, CVS, BLK) and two exits (MSFT, HD). Apply to every position at every review cycle.
