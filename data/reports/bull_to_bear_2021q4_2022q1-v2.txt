    - Rate-shock selloffs differ fundamentally from COVID-panic selloffs and require a different deployment protocol. In COVID panics, quality names maintain bullish MACD (fear is temporary, thesis intact), and RSI extremes below 35 with bullish MACD are clean buying opportunities. In rate-shock selloffs (Fed signals aggressive tightening, growth-to-energy rotation, ALL growth names showing bearish MACD simultaneously), MACD turns bearish broadly because the repricing is fundamental — higher discount rates structurally compress growth multiples, not temporarily. Trigger for this distinction: when 30+ tickers simultaneously show bearish MACD AND energy/commodity names are the only bullish MACD exceptions, the market is in rate-shock mode, not panic mode. In this environment: (1) do NOT deploy into RSI-extreme longs even in highest-conviction names — RSI extreme alone is insufficient when the repricing driver is structural, (2) wait for MACD to begin stabilising or turning before adding new longs, (3) the dual confirmation signal changes to: RSI extreme PLUS MACD turning/flattening, not RSI alone. Rate repricing can persist 4-8 weeks longer than COVID-variant selloffs.
