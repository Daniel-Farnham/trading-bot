    - Week 1 principle: When deploying into a fully-invested bull market from 100% cash, prioritize RSI-based entry quality over momentum. Avoid RSI>75 entries even in high-conviction names — the risk/reward skews unfavorable when already extended. NVDA at RSI=88 and AMD at RSI=84 were passed despite strong AI thesis because entry timing matters for returns.

---
    - Partnership concentration risk in payments networks: A single co-brand relationship (Amazon-Visa) can materially threaten a position even when the macro thesis (digital payments growth) is intact. When a company-specific negative catalyst emerges — especially one that directly threatens a named revenue stream — exit on news confirmation, not after price confirms the damage. The market was already pricing this in via V's RSI=36/bearish technicals before the news broke, suggesting technicals can telegraph fundamental deterioration before it's reported.

---
    - Re-entry discipline after stop-outs: When a position is stopped out on price action but the underlying thesis remains valid, re-entering at a significantly lower price (20%+ discount) with a fresh stop is often superior to staying out entirely. The stop-out proved CRWD's price discipline worked — the thesis didn't break, the market gave us a better entry. Always re-evaluate stopped-out positions for re-entry rather than treating them as permanently closed.

---
    - Early exit vs. catastrophic stop: When a position is within 5% of its catastrophic stop in a deteriorating market environment with bearish trend confirmation (RSI declining, below SMA50, MACD bearish), cutting early with a controlled exit preserves more capital than waiting for the automatic stop. The stop price is a maximum loss limit, not a target exit. PLTR's WEAKENING designation for multiple weeks should have triggered earlier reduction — take action when thesis weakens, not when stop is imminent.

---
    - Overbought positions near Bollinger Band upper boundary (RSI>72) in a volatile, underperforming portfolio are trim candidates even when the thesis is intact. The optimal trade is to recycle capital from extended winners into technically constructive new entries — ABBV at RSI=74/upper BB into NVO at RSI=63/momentum sweet spot is the pattern. Never let 'thesis intact' prevent taking profits at technical extremes; the thesis will still be intact at a lower re-entry point if needed.

Consider whether position size warrants preemptive reduction to manage loss magnitude, especially when MACD is bearish and no fundamental catalyst is visible for recovery.

A CIO-level portfolio should carry at least one energy/commodity position (5-8%) as macro insurance during inflationary regimes. Trigger: CPI sustained above 5% with Fed on hiking path = mandatory review of energy sector exposure. Failure to act = guaranteed sector gap in the strongest performing part of the market.

This prevents reactive exits from fundamentally intact positions on irrelevant headlines.
